$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{x\sin^2 x}{(x\cos x-\sin x)} = \dfrac{0}{0} $ \\

$\overset{(H)}{=} \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{(x\sin^2 x)'}{(x\cos x-\sin x)'}$\\

$= \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{(\sin^2 x+2x\sin x\cos x)}{(\cos x-x\sin x-\cos x)} $\\

$= \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{\sin x( \sin x +2x\cos x)}{-x\sin x} $\\

$= \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{(\sin x +2x\cos x)}{-x} = \dfrac{0}{0} $\\

$\overset{(H)}{=} \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{(\sin x +2x\cos x)'}{(-x)'} $ \\

$= \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{(\cos x+2\cos-2x\sin x)}{-1} $\\

$ = -(1+2-0) = -3$\\
