$\dint_{-1}^{1} \dfrac{dx}{x^2}$\\

Integraal opsplitsen in delen:\\

$\dint_{-1}^{0} \dfrac{dx}{x^2} + \dint_{0}^{1} \dfrac{dx}{x^2}$\\

Linkerintegraal:\\

$\lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0-} \dint_{-1}^{a} \dfrac{dx}{x^2}$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0-} \left(-\dfrac{1}{a}-1 \right) = -\dfrac{1}{0-}-1 = +\infty$\\

Rechterintegraal:\\

$\lim\limits_{b \rightarrow 0+} \dint_{b}^{1} \dfrac{dx}{x^2}$\\

$= \lim\limits_{b \rightarrow 0+} \left(-1+\dfrac{1}{b} \right) = -1+\dfrac{1}{0+} = +\infty$\\

Integraal is divergent!\\
