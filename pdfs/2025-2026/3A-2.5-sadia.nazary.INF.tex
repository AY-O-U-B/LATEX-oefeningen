$f(x)=2x^2-x \quad a \in \{-3,0,2\}$ \\



$f'(a)=\lim\limits_{h\to 0} \dfrac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}$ \\



$f(a+h)=2(a+h)^2-(a+h)$ \\



$=2(a^2+2ah+h^2)-a-h$ \\



$=2a^2+4ah+2h^2-a-h$ \\



$f(a+h)-f(a)=2a^2+4ah+2h^2-a-h-(2a^2-a)$ \\



$=4ah+2h^2-h$ \\



$\dfrac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}=\dfrac{4ah+2h^2-h}{h}$ \\



$=4a+2h-1$ \\



$f'(a)=\lim\limits_{h\to 0}(4a+2h-1)$ \\



$=4a-1$ \\


$f'(-3)=4(-3)-1=-13$ \\



$f'(0)=4(0)-1=-1$ \\



$f'(2)=4(2)-1=7$ \\
