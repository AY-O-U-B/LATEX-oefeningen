$f(x) = \dfrac{1}{2(x-3)^4}+x$\\

$f'(x) = -\dfrac{1}{(2(x-3)^4)^2} \cdot 8(x-3)^3 \cdot 1 +1$\\

$= -\dfrac{1}{4(x-3)^8}\cdot 8(x-3)^3 +1$\\ 

$= -\dfrac{8(x-3)^3}{4(x-3)^8} +1 = -\dfrac{2(x-3)^3}{(x-3)^8} +1$\\

$= -2(x-3)^{-5} +1 = -\dfrac{2}{(x-3)^5} + 1$
