$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 1} \left(\dfrac{\sqrt{x}-x}{x-1}\right) = \dfrac{0}{0}$ (onbepaald) \\

$= \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 1} \left(\dfrac{-\sqrt{x}(-1+\sqrt{x})}{x-1}\right) = \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 1} \left(\dfrac{-\sqrt{x}(\sqrt{x}-1)}{(\sqrt{x}+1)(\sqrt{x}-1)}\right) $ \\

$= \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 1} \left(\dfrac{-\sqrt{x}}{\sqrt{x}-1}\right) = \dfrac{-1}{2}$
