$x^2 + y^2 = 2x(3y^2 - x^2)$\\

$\left \{
	\begin{array}{l}
		x = r\cos\theta\\
		y = r\sin\theta
	\end{array}
\right .$\\

$\Leftrightarrow r^2\cos^2\theta + r^2\sin^2\theta = 2r\cos\theta(3r^2\sin^2\theta-r^2\cos^2\theta)$ \\

$\Leftrightarrow r^2 = 2r\cos\theta\cdot r^2(3\sin^2\theta - \cos^2\theta)$ \\

$\Leftrightarrow r^2 = 2r\cos\theta\cdot r^2(3\sin^2\theta - \cos^2\theta)$ \\

$\Leftrightarrow \dfrac{1}{r} = 2\cos\theta(3\sin^2\theta - \cos^2\theta) \vee r = 0$ \\

$\Leftrightarrow r = \dfrac{1}{2\cos\theta(3\sin^2\theta - \cos^2\theta)} \vee r = 0$ \\
