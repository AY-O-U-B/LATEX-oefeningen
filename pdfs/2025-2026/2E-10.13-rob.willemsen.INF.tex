$5^{2x} + 5^3 = 5^{x + 2} + 5^{x+1}$\\

$\Rightarrow (5^x)^2 + 125 = (5^x \cdot 5^2) + (5^x \cdot 5^1)$\\

$\Rightarrow y^2 + 125 = (y \cdot 25) + (y \cdot 5)$\\

$\Rightarrow y^2 + 125 = 25y + 5y$\\

$\Rightarrow y^2 - 30y + 125 = 0$\\

Discriminant: \\

$\Delta = (-30)^2 - 4 \cdot 1 \cdot 125 = 900 - 500 = 400$\\

$\Rightarrow y = \dfrac{-(-30) \pm \sqrt{400}}{2 \cdot 1} = \dfrac{30 \pm 20}{2}$\\

$\Rightarrow y_1 = \dfrac{30 + 20}{2} = 25$\\

$\Rightarrow y_2 = \dfrac{30 - 20}{2} = 5$\\

y terug substitueren: \\

$\Rightarrow 5^x = 25 \Rightarrow x = \log_5(25) = 2$\\

$\Rightarrow 5^x = 5 \Rightarrow x = \log_5(5) = 1$\\
