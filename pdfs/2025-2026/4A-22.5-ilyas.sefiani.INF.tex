$\displaystyle\int x\sqrt{1-x^2}\operatorname{Bgsin}(x)dx$\\

$\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
u = \operatorname{Bgsin}(x) \\
dv = x\sqrt{1-x^2}dx
\end{array}
\right .
\Rightarrow
\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
du = \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \\
v = -\dfrac{(1-x^2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{3}
\end{array}
\right .$\\

$=-\dfrac{\operatorname{Bgsin}(x)(1-x^2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{3} + \dfrac{1}{3}\displaystyle\int \dfrac{(1-x^2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}dx$\\

$=-\dfrac{\operatorname{Bgsin}(x)(1-x^2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{3} + \dfrac{1}{3}\displaystyle\int (1-x^2)dx$\\

$=-\dfrac{\operatorname{Bgsin}(x)(1-x^2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{3} + \dfrac{1}{3}\left(x-\dfrac{x^3}{3}\right)+C$\\

$=-\dfrac{\operatorname{Bgsin}(x)(1-x^2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{3} + \dfrac{x}{3}-\dfrac{x^3}{9}+C$\\
