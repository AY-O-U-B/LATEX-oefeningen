$\dint \dfrac{1}{x} \ln(\ln x) dx$ \\

$t = \ln x \Rightarrow dt = \dfrac{1}{x} dx$ \\

$\dint \ln t dt$ \\

$ \left\{
\begin{array}{l}
u = \ln t \\
dv = 1dt
\end{array} \right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
du = \dfrac{1}{t} dt \\
v = t
\end{array} 
\right.$ \\

$= t \ln t - \dint t \cdot \dfrac{1}{t} dt$ \\

$= t \ln t - \dint 1 dt$ \\

$= t \ln t - t + C$ \\

$= \ln x \cdot \ln(\ln x) - \ln x + C$ \\
