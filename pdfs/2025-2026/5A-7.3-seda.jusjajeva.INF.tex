$\integral_{0}^{\tfrac{\pi}{4}} \tan xdx$ \\

$\integral \tan xdx$ \\

$= \integral \dfrac{\sin x}{\cos x} dx$ \\

Substitutie: $t = \cos x \Rightarrow dt = -\sin xdx$ \\

$= -\integral \dfrac{1}{t}dt$ \\

$= -\ln \left|\cos x\right| + c$ \\

$\Rightarrow\integral_{0}^{\tfrac{\pi}{4}} \tan xdx = -\ln \left|\cos \dfrac{\pi}{4}\right| + \ln \left|\cos 0\right| = -\ln \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2} + \ln 1 = -\ln \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$ \\
