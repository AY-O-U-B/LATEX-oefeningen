$\dint \sin^2x\cos^2xdx  = \dint\left(\dfrac{1-\cos2x}{2}\right)\left(\dfrac{1+\cos2x}{2}\right)dx$\\

$= \dint \dfrac{1+\cos2x -\cos2x-\cos^22x}{4}dx$\\

$=\dint \dfrac{1-\cos^22x}{4}dx = \dint\dfrac{\sin^22x}{4}dx = \dfrac{1}{4}\dint\sin^22x$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{4}\dint\dfrac{1-\cos4x}{2}dx = \dfrac{1}{8}\dint dx - \dfrac{1}{8}\dint \cos4xdx$\\

$t = 4x \Rightarrow dx = \dfrac{dt}{4}$\\

$\Rightarrow \dfrac{1}{8}x - \dfrac{1}{32}\sin 4x +C$\\
