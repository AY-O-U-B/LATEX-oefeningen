$f(x) = \ln \left(\sqrt{\dfrac{1+x}{1-x}} \right) = \dfrac{1}{2}\ln\left(\dfrac{1+x}{1-x}\right)$\\

$f'(x) = \dfrac{1}{2} \left(\dfrac{1}{\dfrac{1+x}{1-x}} \right)\cdot \dfrac{(1-x)+(1+x)}{(1-x)^2}$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{2}\cdot \dfrac{1}{\dfrac{1+x}{1-x}}\cdot \dfrac{2}{(1-x)^2}$\\

$=  \dfrac{1-x}{1+x}\cdot \dfrac{1}{(1-x)^2}$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{(1+x)(1-x)} = \dfrac{1}{1-x^2}$\\
