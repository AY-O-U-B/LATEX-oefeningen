$\dint\limits_{-\tfrac{1}{2}}^{1} \sqrt{1-x^2}dx$\\

$x = \sin t \Rightarrow t = \Bgsin x \Rightarrow dx = \cos t dt$\\

O.I.:\\

$\dint \cos t \sqrt{1-\sin^2t}dt$\\

$= \dint \cos t \sqrt{\cos^2t}dt$\\

$= \dint \cos^2t dt$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{2}\dint 1 + \cos(2t)dt$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{2}\dint t^0 dt + \dfrac{1}{2}\dint \cos(2t)dt$\\

$= \dfrac{t}{2}+\dfrac{1}{4}\sin(2t)+C$\\

B.I.:\\

$x=1 \Rightarrow t = \Bgsin 1 = \dfrac{\pi}{2}$\\

$x=-\dfrac{1}{2}\Rightarrow t = -\dfrac{\pi}{6}$

$\left[\dfrac{t}{2}+\dfrac{1}{4}\sin(2t)\right]^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}}_{-\tfrac{\pi}{6}}$\\

$=\dfrac{\pi}{4}-\left(-\dfrac{\pi}{12}-\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{8}\right) = \dfrac{3\pi}{12}+\dfrac{\pi}{12}+\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{8} = \dfrac{\pi}{3}+ \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{8}$
