$\dint \dfrac{xdx}{\sqrt{1-x^4}}$ \\

$= \dint \dfrac{xdx}{\sqrt{1-(x^2)^2}}$ \\

$t = x^2 \Rightarrow dt = 2x dx \Rightarrow \dfrac{1}{2}dt = x dx$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \dint \dfrac{dt}{\sqrt{1-t^2}}$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \Bgsin t + C$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \Bgsin x^2 + C$ \\
