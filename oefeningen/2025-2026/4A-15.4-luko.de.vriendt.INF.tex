$\displaystyle \int x^2e^{4x}dx$ \\

$\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     u = x^2 \\
     dv = e^{4x}dx
\end{array}
\right .
\Rightarrow
\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     du = 2xdx \\
     v = \dfrac{e^{4x}}{4}
\end{array}
\right .$ \\

$= \dfrac{x^2e^{4x}}{4} - \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle \int \dfrac{e^{4x}}{\bcancel4} \cdot \bcancel2xdx$ \\

$\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     t = x \\
     dw = e^{4x}dx
\end{array}
\right .
\Rightarrow
\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     dt = dx \\
     w = \dfrac{e^{4x}}{4}
\end{array}
\right .$ \\

$= \dfrac{x^2e^{4x}}{4} - \dfrac{1}{2} \left ( \dfrac{xe^{4x}}{4} - \dfrac{1}{4} \displaystyle \int \dfrac{e^{4x}}{\bcancel4}dx \right )$ \\

$= \dfrac{x^2e^{4x}}{4} - \dfrac{xe^{4x}}{8} + \dfrac{1}{8} \left ( \dfrac{e^{4x}}{4} \right ) + c$ \\

$= \dfrac{x^2e^{4x}}{4} - \dfrac{xe^{4x}}{8} + \dfrac{e^{4x}}{32} + c$ \\
