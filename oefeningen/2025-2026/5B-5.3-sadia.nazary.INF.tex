$\displaystyle \int_{-1}^{0}\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1+x}}\,dx$\\

$=
\displaystyle \lim\limits_{a\to -1+}
\int_a^0\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1+x}}\,dx$\\

$=
\displaystyle \lim\limits_{a\to -1+}
\int_a^0(1+x)^{-\tfrac{1}{2}}\,dx$\\

Stel $u=1+x\Rightarrow du=dx$\\

$x=a\Rightarrow u=1+a$\\

$x=0\Rightarrow u=1$\\

$=
\displaystyle \lim\limits_{a\to -1+}
\int_{1+a}^1 u^{-\tfrac{1}{2}}\,du$\\

$=
\displaystyle \lim\limits_{a\to -1+}
\left[
2\sqrt{u}
\right]_{1+a}^1$\\

$=
\displaystyle \lim\limits_{a\to -1+}
\left(
2\sqrt{1}-2\sqrt{1+a}
\right)$\\

$=
\displaystyle \lim\limits_{a\to -1+}
\left(
2-2\sqrt{1+a}
\right)$\\

$=
2$\\

De oneigenlijke integraal is convergent.\\

$\boxed{2}$\\
