$ r = \sin^2 \dfrac{\theta}{2} $ op het interval $ \left[0, 2\pi \right]  $ \\

$ r^2 = \sin^4 \dfrac{\theta}{2} $ \\

$ r' = 2 \sin \dfrac{\theta}{2} \cos \dfrac{\theta}{2} \cdot \dfrac{1}{2} = \sin \dfrac{\theta}{2} \cos \dfrac{\theta}{2} $ \\

$ (r')^2 = \sin^2 \dfrac{\theta}{2} \cos^2 \dfrac{\theta}{2} $ \\

$ L = \dint_{0}^{2 \pi} \sqrt{\sin^4 \dfrac{\theta}{2} + \sin^2 \dfrac{\theta}{2} \cos^2 \dfrac{\theta}{2}} d\theta  $ \\

$= \dint_{0}^{2 \pi} \sqrt{ \sin^2 \dfrac{\theta}{2} \left( \sin^2 \dfrac{\theta}{2} + \cos^2 \dfrac{\theta}{2} \right)} d\theta  $ \\

$= \dint_{0}^{2 \pi} \sqrt{ \sin^2 \dfrac{\theta}{2} } d\theta  $ \\

$= \dint_{0}^{2 \pi} \sin \dfrac{\theta}{2} d\theta  $ \\

$ u = \dfrac{\theta}{2} \Rightarrow du = \dfrac{1}{2} d\theta \Rightarrow d\theta = 2du $ \\

$= 2 \dint_{0}^{\pi} \sin u du  $ \\

$= -2 \left[ \cos u \right]_{0}^{\pi} $ \\

$= -2 \left[ -1 - 1 \right] $ \\

$= 4 $
