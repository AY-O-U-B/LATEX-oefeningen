$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{x^2}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}dx $ \\

Stel $x = \sin(t) \Rightarrow dx = \cos(t)dt$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\sin^2(t)}{\cos(t)}\cos(t)dt $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int\sin^2(t)dt $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{1-\cos(2t)}{2}dt = \dfrac{1}{2}t - \dfrac{1}{4}\sin(2t) + c $ \\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2}\operatorname{Bgsin}(x) - \dfrac{1}{2}\sin(t)\cos(t) + c $ \\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2}\operatorname{Bgsin}(x) - \dfrac{1}{2}x\sqrt{1-x^2} + c$
