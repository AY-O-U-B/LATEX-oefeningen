$\displaystyle\int_{0}^{1} \ln x dx $\\

$\ln 0$ bestaat niet\\

$\Rightarrow  \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \displaystyle\int_{a}^{1} \ln x dx $\\

$\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
	u = \ln x \\ 
	dv = dx
\end{array}\right.
\Rightarrow 
\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
	du = \dfrac{1}{x} dx\\
 	v = x 
\end{array}
\right.$\\

$\Rightarrow  \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \left (\left[x \ln (x)\right]_{a}^{1} - \displaystyle\int_{a}^{1} x \cdot \dfrac{1}{x} dx\right )$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \left[x \ln (x) - x \right]_{a}^{1} $\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} ((\ln (1) - 1) - (a\ln (a) - a))$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} (- 1 - a\ln (a) + a)$\\

$a\ln(a)$ is onbepaald want $a \rightarrow 0+$ en $\ln(a) \rightarrow -\infty$\\

$a\ln(a) = \dfrac{\ln(a)}{\dfrac{1}{a}}$\\

$\lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \dfrac{\ln(a)}{\dfrac{1}{a}}$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \dfrac{\dfrac{1}{a}}{-\dfrac{1}{a^2}}$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} -a$\\

$= 0$\\

$\Rightarrow \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} (- 1 - a\ln (a) + a)$\\

$= -1 - 0 + 0$\\

$= -1$\\
