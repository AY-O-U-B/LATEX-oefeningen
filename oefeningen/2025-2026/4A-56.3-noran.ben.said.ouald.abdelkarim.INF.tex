$I = \displaystyle\int \sqrt{1-\sin(2x)}dx$\\

$1 = \sin^2x+\cos^2x$\\

$\sin(2x)=2\sin x\cos x$\\

Dus:\\

$1-\sin(2x)=\sin^2x+\cos^2x-2\sin x\cos x = (\sin x-\cos x)^2$\\

$I=\displaystyle\int \sqrt{(\sin x-\cos x)^2}dx =\displaystyle\int |\sin x-\cos x|dx$\\


Geval 1:$\sin x \geq \cos x$\\

$I=\displaystyle\int (\sin x-\cos x)\,dx = -\cos x-\sin x+c$\\

Geval 2: $\sin x \leq \cos x$\\

$I=\displaystyle\int (\cos x-\sin x)\,dx = \sin x+\cos x+c$\\
