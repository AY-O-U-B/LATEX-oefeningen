$f(x) = \dfrac{5x + 1}{9x^2 - 4}$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 0} f(x) = \dfrac{5 \cdot 0 + 1}{9 \cdot 0^2 - 4} = -\dfrac{1}{4}$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -1} f(x) = \dfrac{5(-1) + 1}{9(-1)^2 - 4} = - \dfrac{4}{5} $ \\

$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 1} f(x) = \dfrac{5(1) + 1}{9(1)^2 - 4} = \dfrac{6}{5}$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x \to -\tfrac{2}{3}-} f(x) = -\infty$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x \to -\tfrac{2}{3}+} f(x) = +\infty$ \\
