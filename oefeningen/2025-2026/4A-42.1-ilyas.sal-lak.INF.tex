$\dint \dfrac{dx}{x(x-2)}$ \\

$\dfrac{1}{x(x-2)} = \dfrac{A}{x} + \dfrac{B}{x-2}$ \\

$A = \left.\dfrac{1}{x-2}\right|_{x=0} = -\dfrac{1}{2}$ \quad $B = \left.\dfrac{1}{x}\right|_{x=2} = \dfrac{1}{2}$ \\

$\Rightarrow \dint \dfrac{dx}{x(x-2)} = -\dfrac{1}{2}\ln|x| + \dfrac{1}{2}\ln|x-2| + c = \dfrac{1}{2}\ln\left|\dfrac{x-2}{x}\right| + c$
