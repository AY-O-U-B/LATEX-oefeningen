$f(x) = x+2$, $g(x) = -3x$ en $h(x) = \dfrac{2-x}{3}$\\

Eerst: $f(x) = h(x)$\\

$x+2 = \dfrac{2-x}{3}$\\

$x = -1$\\

$f(-1)=1$\\

$\Rightarrow (-1,1)$\\

Ten tweede: $f(x) = g(x)$\\

$x+2 = -3x$\\

$4x = -2$\\

$x = -\dfrac{1}{2}$\\

$f\left(-\dfrac{1}{2}\right)=\dfrac{3}{2}$\\

$\Rightarrow \left(-\dfrac{1}{2},\dfrac{3}{2}\right)$\\

Ten laatste: $g(x) = h(x)$\\

$-3x = \dfrac{2-x}{3}$\\

$-8x = 2$\\

$x = -\dfrac{1}{4}$\\

$g\left(-\dfrac{1}{4}\right)=\dfrac{3}{4}$\\

$\Rightarrow \left(-\dfrac{1}{4},\dfrac{3}{4}\right)$\\

Wij hebben twee intervallen $\left[-1,-\dfrac{1}{2}\right]$ en $\left[-\dfrac{1}{2},-\dfrac{1}{4}\right]$\\

Vul waarden tussen deze intervallen in en zie welke boven- en onderaan is:\\

Eerste gebied: $x \in \left[-1,-\dfrac{1}{2}\right]$\\

$v_1(x) = f(x)-h(x) = x+2-\dfrac{2-x}{3} = \dfrac{4x+4}{3}$\\

Tweede gebied: $x \in \left[-\dfrac{1}{2},-\dfrac{1}{4}\right]$\\

$v_2(x) = g(x)-h(x) = -3x-\dfrac{2-x}{3} = \dfrac{-8x-2}{3}$\\

$I = \displaystyle\dint_{-1}^{-\tfrac{1}{2}} \left(\dfrac{4x+4}{3}\right)dx + \displaystyle\dint_{-\tfrac{1}{2}}^{-\tfrac{1}{4}} \left(\dfrac{-8x-2}{3}\right)dx$\\

$= \left[\dfrac{2x^2+4x}{3}\right]_{-1}^{-\tfrac{1}{2}} + \left[\dfrac{-4x^2-2x}{3}\right]_{-\tfrac{1}{2}}^{-\tfrac{1}{4}}$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{6}+\dfrac{1}{12} = \dfrac{1}{4}$\\
