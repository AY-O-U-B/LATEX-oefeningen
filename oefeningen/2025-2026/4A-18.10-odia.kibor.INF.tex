$\displaystyle\int  \dfrac{x}{\cos^2x}dx$\\

$\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
	u = x \\ 
	dv = \dfrac{1}{\cos^2x}dx
\end{array}\right.
\Rightarrow 
\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
	du = dx\\
 	v = \tan x
\end{array}
\right.$\\

$\displaystyle\int  \dfrac{x}{\cos^2x}dx$\\

$= x \tan x - \displaystyle\int \tan x dx$ \\

$= x \tan x - \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\sin x}{\cos x} dx$ \\

$u = \cos x \Rightarrow du = - \sin xdx$\\

$= x \tan x + \displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{u} dx$ \\

$= x \tan x + \ln |\cos x| + C$ \\
