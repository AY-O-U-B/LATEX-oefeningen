$\displaystyle \int \dfrac{\sin (2x)}{1 + \sin^2 (x)}dx$ \\

$=\displaystyle \int \dfrac{\sin (2x)}{1 + \dfrac{1 - \cos (2x)}{2}}dx$ \\

$=2\displaystyle \int \dfrac{\sin (2x)}{3 - \cos (2x)}dx$ \\

$u = 3 - \cos (2x)\Rightarrow du = 2\sin (2x)dx$ \\

$= \displaystyle \int \dfrac{du}{u}$ \\

$= \ln |3-\cos (2x)|+c$ \\

OF \\

$\displaystyle \int \dfrac{\sin (2x)}{1 + \sin^2 (x)}dx$ \\

$=\displaystyle \int \dfrac{2\sin (x) \cos (x)}{1 + \sin^2 (x)}dx$ \\

$u = \sin (x) \Rightarrow du = \cos (x)dx$ \\

$=2\displaystyle \int \dfrac{udu}{1 + u^2}$ \\

$v = 1 + u^2 \Rightarrow dv = 2udu$ \\

$=\displaystyle \int \dfrac{dv}{v}$ \\

$=\ln |1 + \sin^2 (x)| + c$ \\
