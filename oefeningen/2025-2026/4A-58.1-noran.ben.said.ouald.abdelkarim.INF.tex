$I = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{1-\sin x}dx$\\

Stel $ t=\tan\left(\dfrac{x}{2}\right)$\\

$ \sin x=\dfrac{2t}{1+t^2} \Rightarrow dx=\dfrac{2}{1+t^2}dt$\\

Dus:\\

$ I=\displaystyle\int\dfrac{1}{1-\dfrac{2t}{1+t^2}}\cdot\dfrac{2}{1+t^2}dt=\displaystyle\int\dfrac{1+t^2}{1+t^2-2t}\cdot\dfrac{2}{1+t^2}dt$\\

$ =\displaystyle\int\dfrac{1+t^2}{(t-1)^2}\cdot\dfrac{2}{1+t^2}dt=\displaystyle\int\dfrac{2}{(t-1)^2}dt$\\

$ =2\displaystyle\int (t-1)^{-2}dt=2\cdot\dfrac{(t-1)^{-1}}{-1}+c=\dfrac{-2}{t-1}+c$\\

Terug substitueren:\\

$ I=\dfrac{-2}{\tan\left(\dfrac{x}{2}\right)-1}+c$\\
