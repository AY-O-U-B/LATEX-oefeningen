$ \displaystyle\int_0^1 \ln x dx $\\

$ = \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \displaystyle\int_a^1 \ln x dx $\\

$ \left \{
\begin{array}{l}
u = \ln x \\
dv = dx
\end{array}
\right .
\Rightarrow
\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
du = \dfrac{1}{x} dx \\
v = x
\end{array}
\right . $\\

$ = \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \left( x \ln x-\displaystyle\int x\cdot\dfrac{1}{x} dx \right)_a^1 $\\

$ = \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \left( x \ln x-\displaystyle\int 1 dx \right)_a^1 $\\

$ = \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \left( x \ln x-x \right)_a^1 $\\

$ = \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \left[ (1\cdot\ln(1)-1)-(a\ln(a)-a) \right] $\\

$ = \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} (-1-a\ln(a)+a) $\\

$ \left \{
\begin{array}{l}
\lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} a\ln(a) = 0\cdot (-\infty) \text{ onbepaald} \\

\Rightarrow \dfrac{\ln(a)}{\dfrac{1}{a}} = \dfrac{-\infty}{+\infty} \overset{\text{L'Hôpital}}{=} \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \dfrac{\dfrac{1}{a}}{-\dfrac{1}{a^2}} = \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} \dfrac{1}{a}\cdot(-a^2) = \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0+} -a = 0
\end{array}
\right . $\\

$ = -1-0+0 $\\

$ = -1 $\\

$ \displaystyle\int_0^1 \ln x dx=-1 $
