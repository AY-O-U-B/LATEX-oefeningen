Bereken de volgende limiet: \\

$ \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -\tfrac{1}{2}} \dfrac{-6x^2 - x + 1}{-10x^2 - x + 2} = \cancel{\dfrac{0}{0}}$\\

Teller ontbinden: \\

$ -6x^2 - x + 1 = -(6x^2 + x - 1) $ \\

$ \Rightarrow -6x^2 - x + 1 = -(3x - 1)(2x + 1) $ \\

Noemer ontbinden: \\

$ -10x^2 - x + 2 = -(10x^2 + x - 2) $\\

$ \Rightarrow -10x^2 - x + 2 = -(5x - 2)(2x + 1) $\\

$ \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -\tfrac{1}{2}} \dfrac{\cancel{(2x+1)} (1 - 3x)}{\cancel{(2x+1)} (2 - 5x)} $ \\

$ = \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -\tfrac{1}{2}} \dfrac{1 - 3x}{2 - 5x} $ \\

Invullen: \\

$ = \dfrac{1 - 3\left(-\dfrac{1}{2}\right)}{2 - 5\left(-\dfrac{1}{2}\right)} = \dfrac{5}{9} $ \\
