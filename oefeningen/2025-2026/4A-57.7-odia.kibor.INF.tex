$ \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\tan x dx}{1 + \cos^2 x} $\\

$t = \tan x$\\

$dt = (1 + t^2) dx$\\

$\dfrac{dt}{1 + t^2} = dx$\\

$\Rightarrow \displaystyle\int \dfrac{t}{1 + \dfrac{1}{1 + t^2}} \cdot \dfrac{1}{1 + t^2} dt $\\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{t}{\dfrac{2 + t^2}{1 + t^2}} \cdot \dfrac{1}{1 + t^2} dt $\\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{t}{2 + t^2} dt $\\

$u = 2 + t^2$\\

$du = 2t dt$\\

$\dfrac{du}{2} = t dt$\\

$\Rightarrow  \dfrac{1}{2}\displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{u} du $\\

$= \dfrac{\ln |2 + \tan^2 x|}{2} + C$\\
