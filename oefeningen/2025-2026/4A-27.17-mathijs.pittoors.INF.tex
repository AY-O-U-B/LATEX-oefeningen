$\intt \dfrac{x^4 + x^3 + 3x^2 + x + 1}{x^3+1}dx$\\

Euclidische deling:\\


\begin{tabular}{lllll|ll}
$x^4$ & $+x^3$ & $+3x^2$ & $+x$ & $+1$ & $x^3$ & $+1$\\ \cline{6-7}
$-x^4$& & & $-x$ && $x$ & $+1$\\ \cline{1-5}
& $x^3$ & $3x^2$ & & $ +1$\\
& $-x^3$ & & & $ -1$\\ \cline{1-5}
& & $3x^2$ & &\\
\end{tabular}\\

$\Rightarrow \intt x + 1 dx + \int \dfrac{3x^2dx}{x^3 + 1} = \dfrac{x^2}{2} + x + \int \dfrac{3x^2dx}{x^3 + 1}$\\

Neem $t = x^3 + 1$ dan $dt = 3x^2 dx$ dus $dx = \dfrac{dt}{3x^2}$\\

dan $\dfrac{x^2}{2} + x + \intt \dfrac{dt}{t} = \dfrac{x^2}{2} + x + \ln |x^3 + 1| + c$ met $c \in \mathbb{R}$
