$\displaystyle\int e^x \cos (x) dx$\\

$\left\{\begin{array}{l}
u = \cos (x)\\
dv=e^x dx
\end{array}
\Rightarrow
\left\{\begin{array}{l}
du = - \sin(x) dx\\
v=e^x
\end{array}\right.\right.$\\

$\displaystyle \int e^x\cos(x)dx = e^x\cos (x)- \displaystyle \int e^x(-\sin(x))dx$\\

$= e^x\cos (x)+ \displaystyle \int e^x\sin (x)dx$\\

$J=\displaystyle \int e^x\sin (x)dx$\\

$\left\{\begin{array}{l}
u=\sin (x)\\
dv=e^x dx
\end{array}
\Rightarrow
\left\{\begin{array}{l}
du=\cos (x)dx\\
v=e^x
\end{array}\right.\right.$\\

$=e^x\sin (x)- \displaystyle \int e^x\cos (x)dx$\\

$J=e^x\sin (x)-I$\\

$I=e^x\cos (x)+J$\\

$I=e^x\cos (x)+e^x\sin (x)-I$\\

$2I=e^x\cos (x)+e^x\sin (x) + C$\\

$I=\dfrac{e^x}{2}\left(\cos (x)+\sin (x)\right)+C$
