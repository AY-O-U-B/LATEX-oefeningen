\noindent Bereken de tweede afgeleide van $f$\\

$f(x) = \sin(x) \cos(x)$\\

$f'(x) = \dfrac{d \sin(x)}{dx} \cos(x) + \dfrac{d \cos(x)}{dx} \sin(x) = \cos^2(x) - \sin^2(x)$\\

$f''(x) = \dfrac{d(\cos^2(x) - \sin^2(x))}{dx} = \dfrac{d(\cos^2(x))}{dx} - \dfrac{d(\sin^2(x))}{dx} = $\\

$-2\cos(x)\sin(x) - 2\sin(x)\cos(x) = - 4 \cos(x)\sin(x) = -2 \sin(2x)$\\
