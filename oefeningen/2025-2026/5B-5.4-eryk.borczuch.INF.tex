$\displaystyle \int_{0}^1 \ln(x)dx$\\

$\displaystyle = \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0^+} \int_{a}^1 \ln(x)dx$\\

$ = \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0^+} \left[x\ln(x) - x\right]_a^1$\\

$\displaystyle = \lim_{a \rightarrow 0^+} \left(-1 - a\ln(a) + a\right)$\\


$ \lim \limits_{a \rightarrow 0^+} a\ln(a) 
= \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0^+} \dfrac{\ln(a)}{\dfrac{1}{a}} 
\stackrel{H}{=} \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0^+} \dfrac{\dfrac{1}{a}}{-\dfrac{1}{a^2}} 
= \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow 0^+} (-a) = 0$\\

$\displaystyle = -1 - 0 + 0 = -1$\\
