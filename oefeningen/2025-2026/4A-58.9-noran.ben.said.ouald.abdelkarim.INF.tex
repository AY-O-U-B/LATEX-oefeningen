$ I = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{\cos x-\sin x+1}dx$\\

Stel $ t=\tan\left(\dfrac{x}{2}\right)$\\

$ \sin x=\dfrac{2t}{1+t^2}$\\

$ \cos x=\dfrac{1-t^2}{1+t^2}$\\

$ dx=\dfrac{2}{1+t^2}dt$\\

Dus:\\

$ I=\displaystyle\int\dfrac{1}{\dfrac{1-t^2}{1+t^2}-\dfrac{2t}{1+t^2}+1}\cdot \dfrac{2}{1+t^2}dt$\\

$ =\displaystyle\int\dfrac{1}{\dfrac{1-t^2-2t+1+t^2}{1+t^2}}\cdot\dfrac{2}{1+t^2}dt=\displaystyle\int\dfrac{1+t^2}{2-2t}\cdot\dfrac{2}{1+t^2}dt$\\

$ =\displaystyle\int\dfrac{1}{1-t}dt$\\

$ =-\ln|1-t|+c$\\

Terug substitueren:\\

$ I=-\ln\left|1-\tan\left(\dfrac{x}{2}\right)\right|+c$\\
