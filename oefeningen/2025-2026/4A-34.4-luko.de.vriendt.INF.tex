$\displaystyle \int e^{\sin (x)} \cos^3 (x)dx$ \\

$= -\displaystyle \int e^{\sin (x)} \cos (x) (\sin^2 (x) -1)dx$ \\

$\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     u = \sin (x) \\
     du = \cos (x)dx
\end{array}
\right .$ \\

$= -\displaystyle \int e^u(u^2 - 1)du$ \\

$\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     v = u^2 - 1\\
     dw = e^udu
\end{array}
\right .
\Rightarrow
\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     dv = 2udu \\
     w = e^u
\end{array}
\right .$ \\

$= -\left ( e^u(u^2 - 1) - 2\displaystyle \int ue^u du \right)$ \\

$= -e^u(u^2 - 1) + 2\left( ue^u - \displaystyle \int e^u du\right)$ \\

$\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     t = u \\
     dw = e^udu
\end{array}
\right .
\Rightarrow
\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     dt = du \\
     w = e^u
\end{array}
\right .$ \\

$= -e^u(u^2 - 1) + 2ue^u -2e^u + c$ \\

$= -e^uu^2 \cancel{+ e^u} + 2ue^u - \bcancel{2} e^u + c$ \\

$= -\sin^2 (x)e^{\sin(x)} + 2\sin (x)e^{\sin (x)} - e^{\sin (x)} + c$ \\
