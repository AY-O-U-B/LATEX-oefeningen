Zij $g(x,y) = e^x\sin y$ en zij $f(x,y) = (xy^2,x^2y)$\\

Eerst voor $g(x,y)$:\\

$\dfrac{\partial g}{\partial x} = e^x\sin y$\\

$\dfrac{\partial g}{\partial y} = e^x\cos y$\\

Daarna voor $f(x,y)$:\\

$\dfrac{\partial f_1}{\partial x} = y^2$\\

$\dfrac{\partial f_2}{\partial x} = 2xy$\\

$\dfrac{\partial f_1}{\partial y} = 2xy$\\

$\dfrac{\partial f_2}{\partial y} = x^2$\\

$Dg(x,y)=\left(\begin{array}{cc}
e^x\sin y & e^x\cos y
\end{array}\right)$\\

$Df(x,y)=\left(\begin{array}{cc}
y^2 & 2xy \\
2xy & x^2
\end{array}\right)$\\

Vul $f(x,y) = (xy^2,x^2y)$ in $g(x,y)$:\\

$Dg(f(x,y))=\left(\begin{array}{cc}
e^{xy^2}\sin (x^2y) & e^{xy^2}\cos (x^2y)
\end{array}\right)$\\

Nu de kettingregel toepassen:\\

$D(g\circ f)(x,y) = \left(\begin{array}{cc}
e^{xy^2}\left(y^2\sin(x^2y)+2xy\cos(x^2y)\right) & e^{xy^2}\left(2xy\sin(x^2y)+x^2\cos(x^2y)\right)
\end{array}\right)$\\
