$ \dint_{0}^{\sqrt[4]{3}} \dfrac{2x}{1+x^4} dx $ \\

stel $t = x^2 \Rightarrow dt = 2x dx $ \\

als  $ x = 0 \Rightarrow t = 0^2 = 0 $ \\

$ x = \sqrt[4]{3} \Rightarrow t = (\sqrt[4]{3})^2 = \sqrt{3} $ \\

$ \int_{0}^{\sqrt{3}} \dfrac{1}{1+t^2} dt $ \\

$ = \left[\operatorname{Bgtan}(t) \right]_{0}^{\sqrt{3}} $ \\

$ = \dfrac{\pi}{3} - 0 = \dfrac{\pi}{3} $ \\
