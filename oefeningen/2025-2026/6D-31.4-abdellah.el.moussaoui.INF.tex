$\displaystyle\sum_{n = 2}^{\infty}\dfrac{\ln n}{n^2}$\\

Laat ons het convergentiegedrag controleren van: $\displaystyle\int^{+\infty}_2\dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}dx$\\

$ \lim\limits_{a\to+\infty} \displaystyle\int^a_2 \dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}dx$\\

$=\lim\limits_{a\to+\infty} \left[-\dfrac{\ln x}{x}\right]^a_2-\displaystyle\int^a_2\dfrac{1}{x^2}dx$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a\to+\infty} \left[-\dfrac{\ln x}{x} - \dfrac{1}{x}\right]^a_2$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a\to+\infty} \dfrac{-\ln a}{a}-\dfrac{1}{a} + \dfrac{\ln2+1}{2}$\\

$= \dfrac{\ln2+1}{2}$\\

Dus $\displaystyle\int^{+\infty}_2\dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}dx$ is convergent, vervolgens is $\displaystyle\sum_{n = 2}^{\infty}\dfrac{\ln n}{n^2}$ convergent volgens het integraalcriterium.\\
