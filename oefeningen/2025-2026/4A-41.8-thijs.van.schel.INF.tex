$\dint \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{x^2+x+6}}$ \\

$= \dint \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{(x^2+x+\dfrac{1}{4}) - \dfrac{1}{4} + 6}}$ \\

$= \dint \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{(x+\dfrac{1}{2})^2 + \dfrac{23}{4}}}$ \\

$t = x+\dfrac{1}{2} \Rightarrow dt = dx$ \\

$= \dint \dfrac{dt}{\sqrt{t^2 + \dfrac{23}{4}}}$ \\

$= \ln \left| t + \sqrt{t^2 + \dfrac{23}{4}} \right| + C$ \\

$= \ln \left| x + \dfrac{1}{2} + \sqrt{(x+\dfrac{1}{2})^2 + \dfrac{23}{4}} \right| + C$ \\

$= \ln \left| x + \dfrac{1}{2} + \sqrt{x^2+x+6} \right| + C$
