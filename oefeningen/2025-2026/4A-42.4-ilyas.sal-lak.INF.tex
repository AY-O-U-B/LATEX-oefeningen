$\dint \dfrac{2x+1}{x^2-1} \, dx$ \\

$x^2 - 1 = (x-1)(x+1)$ \\

$\dfrac{2x+1}{(x-1)(x+1)} = \dfrac{A}{x-1} + \dfrac{B}{x+1}$ \\

$A = \left.\dfrac{2x+1}{x+1}\right|_{x=1} = \dfrac{3}{2}$ \quad $B = \left.\dfrac{2x+1}{x-1}\right|_{x=-1} = \dfrac{1}{2}$ \\

$\Rightarrow \dint \dfrac{2x+1}{x^2-1} \, dx = \dfrac{3}{2}\ln|x-1| + \dfrac{1}{2}\ln|x+1| + c$
