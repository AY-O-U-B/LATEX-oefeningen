$f(x) = \ln\left(\dfrac{1+\cos ax}{1- \cos ax} \right)^{\tfrac{1}{2}}$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{2}\left(\ln(1+\cos ax) - \ln(1-\cos ax)\right)$\\

$f'(x) = \dfrac{1}{2}\left( \dfrac{-a\sin ax}{1+\cos ax} - \dfrac{a\sin ax}{ 1-\cos ax} \right)$\\

$ = \dfrac{-a\sin ax}{2}\left( \dfrac{1}{1+\cos ax} + \dfrac{1}{ 1-\cos ax} \right)$\\

$ = \dfrac{-a\sin ax}{2}\left( \dfrac{(1-\cos ax) + (1+ \cos ax)}{(1+\cos ax)(1-\cos ax)}  \right)$\\

$ = \dfrac{-a\sin ax}{2}\left( \dfrac{2}{\sin^2 ax}  \right)$\\

$ = \dfrac{-a}{\sin ax}$\\
