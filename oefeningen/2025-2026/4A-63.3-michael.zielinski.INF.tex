\normalsize

$ \displaystyle\int\sqrt{(1-x^2)^3}dx $ \\

Neem: \\

$ x = \cos t $ \\

$ dx = -\sin t dt $ \\

Dan: \\

$ \displaystyle\int\sqrt{(1-x^2)^3}dx
= -\displaystyle\int\sqrt{(1-\cos^2 t)^3}\sin t dt $ \\

$ = -\displaystyle\int\sqrt{\sin^6 t}\sin t dt $ \\

$ = -\displaystyle\int\sin^3 t \sin t dt $ \\

$ = -\displaystyle\int\sin^4 t dt $ \\

$ = -\displaystyle\int\left(\dfrac{1-\cos 2t}{2}\right)^2 dt $ \\

$ = -\dfrac{1}{4}\displaystyle\int(1-2\cos 2t+\cos^2 2t)dt $ \\

$ = -\dfrac{1}{4}\displaystyle\int 1dt
+ \dfrac{1}{2}\displaystyle\int\cos 2t dt
- \dfrac{1}{4}\displaystyle\int\cos^2 2t dt $ \\

$ = -\dfrac{1}{4}t
+ \dfrac{1}{4}\sin 2t
- \dfrac{1}{4}\displaystyle\int\dfrac{1+\cos 4t}{2}dt $ \\

$ = -\dfrac{1}{4}t
+ \dfrac{1}{4}\sin 2t
- \dfrac{1}{8}\displaystyle\int(1+\cos 4t)dt $ \\

$ = -\dfrac{1}{4}t
+ \dfrac{1}{4}\sin 2t
- \dfrac{1}{8}t
- \dfrac{1}{32}\sin 4t + c $ \\

$ = -\dfrac{3}{8}t
+ \dfrac{1}{4}\sin 2t
- \dfrac{1}{32}\sin 4t + c $ \\

Terug substitueren: \\

$ t = \operatorname{Bgcos}x $ \\

$ = -\dfrac{3}{8}\operatorname{Bgcos}x
+ \dfrac{1}{4}\sin(2\operatorname{Bgcos}x)
- \dfrac{1}{32}\sin(4\operatorname{Bgcos}x) + c $ \\
