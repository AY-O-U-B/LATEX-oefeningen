$\displaystyle\int x^2 \cos^2 x dx = \displaystyle\int x^2 \cdot \dfrac{1 + \cos 2x}{2} dx$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int x^2 dx + \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int x^2 \cos 2x dx$\\

$\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
	u = x^2 \\ 
	dv = \cos 2x dx
\end{array}\right.
\Rightarrow 
\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
	du = 2x dx\\
 	v = \dfrac{1}{2} \sin 2x 
\end{array}
\right.$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \cdot \dfrac{x^3}{3} + \dfrac{1}{2} \left(\dfrac{x^2}{2} \sin 2x -\displaystyle\int x \sin 2x dx \right)$\\

$\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
	u = x \\ 
	dv = \sin 2x dx
\end{array}\right.
\Rightarrow 
\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
	du = dx\\
 	v = -\dfrac{1}{2} \cos 2x 
\end{array}
\right.$\\

$= \dfrac{x^3}{6} + \dfrac{x^2}{4} \sin 2x - \dfrac{1}{2} \left(-\dfrac{x}{2} \cos 2x -\displaystyle\int -\dfrac{1}{2} \cos 2x dx \right)$\\

$= \dfrac{x^3}{6} + \dfrac{x^2}{4} \sin 2x - \dfrac{1}{2} \left(-\dfrac{x}{2} \cos 2x + \dfrac{1}{4} \sin 2x \right)$\\

$= \dfrac{x^3}{6} + \dfrac{x^2}{4} \sin 2x + \dfrac{x}{4} \cos 2x - \dfrac{1}{8} \sin 2x + C$\\
