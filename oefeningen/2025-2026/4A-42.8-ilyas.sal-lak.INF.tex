$\dint \dfrac{dx}{x^3 - 7x + 6}$ \\

$x^3 - 7x + 6 = (x-1)(x-2)(x+3)$ \\

$\dfrac{1}{(x-1)(x-2)(x+3)} = \dfrac{A}{x-1} + \dfrac{B}{x-2} + \dfrac{C}{x+3}$ \\

$A = \left.\dfrac{1}{(x-2)(x+3)}\right|_{x=1} = \dfrac{1}{(-1)(4)} = -\dfrac{1}{4}$ \\

$B = \left.\dfrac{1}{(x-1)(x+3)}\right|_{x=2} = \dfrac{1}{(1)(5)} = \dfrac{1}{5}$ \\

$C = \left.\dfrac{1}{(x-1)(x-2)}\right|_{x=-3} = \dfrac{1}{(-4)(-5)} = \dfrac{1}{20}$ \\

$\Rightarrow \dint \dfrac{dx}{x^3-7x+6} = -\dfrac{1}{4}\ln|x-1| + \dfrac{1}{5}\ln|x-2| + \dfrac{1}{20}\ln|x+3| + c$
