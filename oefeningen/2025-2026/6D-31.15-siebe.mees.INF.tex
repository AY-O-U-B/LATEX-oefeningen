$\displaystyle\sum_{n \geq 2}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{(\ln n)^{\ln n}} = \sum_{n \geq 2}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{e^{\ln n \ln(\ln n)}} = \sum_{n \geq 2}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{n^{\ln(\ln n)}}$\\

$\Rightarrow \displaystyle\sum_{n \geq 2}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{n^{\ln(\ln n)}} \leq \displaystyle\sum_{n \geq 2}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{n^2}$\\

$\displaystyle\sum_{n \geq 2}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{n^2}$ convergent $\Rightarrow \displaystyle\sum_{n \geq 2}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{n^{\ln(\ln n)}}$ ook.\\
