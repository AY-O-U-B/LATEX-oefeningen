$\integral\dfrac{\sin x + x\cos x}{x\sin x}dx$ \\

$= \integral\dfrac{\sin x}{x\sin x}dx + \integral\dfrac{x\cos x}{x\sin x}dx$ \\

$= \integral\dfrac{1}{x}dx + \integral\dfrac{\cos x}{\sin x}dx$ \\

$= \ln |x| + \displaystyle\int\dfrac{\cos x}{\sin x}dx$ \\

Substitutie: $t = \sin x \Rightarrow dt = \cos xdx$ \\

$= \ln |x| + \integral\dfrac{1}{t}dt$ \\

$= \ln |x| + \ln |t| + c$ \\

$= \ln |x| + \ln |\sin x| + c$ \\
