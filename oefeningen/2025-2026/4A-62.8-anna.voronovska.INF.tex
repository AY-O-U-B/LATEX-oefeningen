$\displaystyle\int\dfrac{dx}{x^2\sqrt{x^2-4}}$\\

Vorm $\sqrt{x^2-a^2}$ met $a=2$, stel $x = \dfrac{2}{\cos t} \Rightarrow dx = \dfrac{2\sin t}{\cos^2 t}dt$\\

$\sqrt{x^2-4} = \sqrt{\dfrac{4}{\cos^2 t}-4} = \dfrac{2\sin t}{\cos t}$\\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{\dfrac{4}{\cos^2 t} \cdot \dfrac{2\sin t}{\cos t}} \cdot \dfrac{2\sin t}{\cos^2 t}dt$\\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\cos^3 t}{8\sin t} \cdot \dfrac{2\sin t}{\cos^2 t}dt$\\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\cos t}{4}dt$\\

$= \dfrac{\sin t}{4} + c$\\

Terugsubstitutie: $\cos t = \dfrac{2}{x}$, $\sin t = \dfrac{\sqrt{x^2-4}}{x}$\\

$= \dfrac{\sqrt{x^2-4}}{4x} + c$
