$\integral_{-1}^{1} \dfrac{dx}{x^2}$ \\

$\integral \dfrac{1}{x^2}dx = -\dfrac{1}{x} + c$ \\

$\Rightarrow\integral_{-1}^{1} \dfrac{dx}{x^2} = \integral_{-1}^{0} \dfrac{dx}{x^2} + \integral_{0}^{1} \dfrac{dx}{x^2}$ \\

$= \limit_{a\to 0-} \integral_{-1}^{a} \dfrac{dx}{x^2} + \limit_{a\rightarrow 0+} \integral_{a}^{1} \dfrac{dx}{x^2}$ \\

$= \limit_{a\to 0-} \left[-\dfrac{1}{x}\right]_{-1}^{a} + \limit_{a\to 0+} \left[-\dfrac{1}{x}\right]_{a}^{1}$ \\

$= \limit_{a\to 0-} \left(-\dfrac{1}{a} + \dfrac{1}{-1}\right) + \limit_{a\to 0+} \left(-\dfrac{1}{1} + \dfrac{1}{a}\right)$ \\

$= \limit_{a\to 0-} \left(-\dfrac{1}{a} - 1\right) + \limit_{a\to 0+} \left(-1 + \dfrac{1}{a}\right)$ \\

$= \left(- (-\infty) - 1\right) + \left(-1 + (+\infty)\right)$ \\

$= +\infty + (+\infty)$ \\

$= +\infty$ (divergent, integraal bestaat niet) \\
