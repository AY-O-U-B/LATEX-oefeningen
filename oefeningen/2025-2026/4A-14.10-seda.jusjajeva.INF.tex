$\integral \ln(x^x) dx$ \\

$= \integral x\ln x dx$ \\

Partiële integratie: $\left\{\begin{array}{l}
    u = \ln x \\ 
    dv = xdx 
\end{array}\right. \Rightarrow\left\{\begin{array}{l}
    du = \dfrac{dx}{x} \\
    v = \dfrac{x^2}{2} 
\end{array}\right.$ \\

$= \dfrac{x^2 \ln x}{2} - \integral \dfrac{x^2}{2x}dx$ \\

$= \dfrac{x^2 \ln x}{2} - \dfrac{1}{2}\integral xdx$ \\

$= \dfrac{x^2 \ln x}{2} - \dfrac{x^2}{4} + c$ \\

$= \dfrac{x^2 (2\ln x - 1)}{4} + c$ \\
