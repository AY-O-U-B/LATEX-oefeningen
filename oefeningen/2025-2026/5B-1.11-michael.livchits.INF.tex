$I = \displaystyle\dint_{-\infty}^{1} \dfrac{dx}{x^3}$\\

Eerst vinden we de onbepaalde integraal\\

$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{x^3} = -\dfrac{1}{2x^2} + C$\\

Neem de limiet\\

$I = \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow 0-}\left[-\dfrac{1}{2x^2}\right]_{-\infty}^{b} + \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow 0+}\left[-\dfrac{1}{2x^2}\right]_{b}^{1}$\\

Eerst linker limiet:\\

$I_1 = \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow 0-}\left[-\dfrac{1}{2x^2}\right]_{-\infty}^{b} = \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow 0-}\left(-\dfrac{1}{2b^2} + 0\right) = -\infty$\\

Nu rechter limiet:\\

$I_2 = \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow 0+}\left[-\dfrac{1}{2x^2}\right]_{b}^{1} = \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow 0+}\left(-\dfrac{1}{2} + \dfrac{1}{2b^2}\right) = +\infty$\\

$\Rightarrow$ divergeert\\
