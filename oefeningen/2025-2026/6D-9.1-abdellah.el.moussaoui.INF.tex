$s_n = \displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{n} \dfrac{1}{k\left(k+1\right)}$\\


$\Rightarrow \dfrac{1}{k\left(k+1\right)} = \dfrac{1}{k}-\dfrac{1}{k+1}$ \\

$s_n = \displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{n}\left(\dfrac{1}{k}-\dfrac{1}{k+1}\right)$\\

$= \left(1-\dfrac{1}{2}\right) + \left(\dfrac{1}{2} - \dfrac{1}{3}\right) + \left(\dfrac{1}{3} - \dfrac{1}{4}\right)+\cdots+\left(\dfrac{1}{n} - \dfrac{1}{n+1}\right)$\\

$= 1-\dfrac{1}{n+1}$ \\

$\lim\limits_{n\to+\infty} s_n=\lim\limits_{n\to+\infty}\dfrac{n}{n+1} = 1$ \\

Dus de reeks is convergent en de som is $1$.\\
