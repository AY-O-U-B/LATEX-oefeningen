$\displaystyle \int \dfrac{x-1}{\sqrt{x+2}}dx$\\

stel: $t = \sqrt{x+2}$\\

$\Leftrightarrow x = t^2-2$\\

$\dfrac{dt}{dx} = (\sqrt{x+2})'$\\

$\Leftrightarrow dx = 2t dt$\\

$\displaystyle \int \dfrac{x-1}{\sqrt{x+2}}dx = \int(2t^2 -6 )dt$\\

$ = \dfrac{2}{3}t^3 -6t +c $\\

$= \dfrac{2}{3}(x+2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}-6 \sqrt{x+2}+c$
