$\displaystyle\int x\sin^2 (x) dx$\\


$= \displaystyle \int x\cdot \dfrac{1-\cos 2x}{2} dx$\\

$= \displaystyle \dfrac{1}{2} \int x dx - \dfrac{1}{2}\int x\cos (2x)dx$\\

$\left\{\begin{array}{l}
u=x\\
dv=\cos (2x)dx
\end{array}
\Rightarrow
\left\{\begin{array}{l}
du=dx\\
v=\dfrac{\sin 2x}{2}
\end{array}\right.\right.$\\

$\displaystyle
\int x \cos (2x)dx
=
\dfrac{x}{2}\sin 2x-\dfrac{1}{2}\int \sin 2x dx$\\

$\displaystyle
=
\dfrac{x}{2}\sin 2x + \dfrac{\cos 2x}{4}$\\

$\displaystyle
\int x\sin^2 xdx
=
\dfrac{x^2}{4}
-\dfrac{1}{2}\left(\dfrac{x}{2}\sin 2x + \dfrac14\cos 2x\right)+C$\\

$\displaystyle
=
\dfrac{x^2}{4}-\dfrac{x}{4}\sin 2x - \dfrac{\cos 2x}{8} + C$
