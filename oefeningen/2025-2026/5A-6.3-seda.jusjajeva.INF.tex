$\integral_0^2 \dfrac{2x}{x^2 + 1}dx$ \\

$= \integral \dfrac{2x}{x^2 + 1}dx$ \\

$= 2\integral \dfrac{x}{x^2 + 1}dx$ \\

Substitutie: $t = x^2 + 1 \Rightarrow dt = 2xdx$ \\

$= \integral \dfrac{1}{t}dt$ \\

$= \ln\left|x^2 + 1\right| + c$ \\

$\Rightarrow \integral_0^2 \dfrac{2x}{x^2 + 1}dx = \left[\ln\left|x^2 + 1\right|\right]_0^2 = \ln \left|2^2 + 1\right| - \ln \left|0^2 + 1\right| = \ln 5 - \ln 1 = \ln 5$ \\
