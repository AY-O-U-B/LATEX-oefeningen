$\displaystyle \int \dfrac{dx}{x^2-4}$\\

$\displaystyle =\int \dfrac{dx}{(x-2)(x+2)}$\\

$\dfrac{1}{(x-2)(x+2)} = \dfrac{A}{x-2} + \dfrac{B}{x+2}$\\


$\displaystyle A=\left.\dfrac{1}{x+2}\right|_{x=2}=\dfrac{1}{4}$\\

$\displaystyle B =\left.\dfrac{1}{x-2}\right|_{x=-2}=\dfrac{-1}{4}$\\


$\displaystyle \int \dfrac{dx}{(x-2)(x+2)} = \dfrac{1}{4} \int \dfrac{dx}{x-2} - \dfrac{1}{4} \int \dfrac{dx}{x+2}$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{4} \ln |x-2| - \dfrac{1}{4} \ln |x+2| +c$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{4} \ln \left|\dfrac{x-2}{x+2}\right| +c$
