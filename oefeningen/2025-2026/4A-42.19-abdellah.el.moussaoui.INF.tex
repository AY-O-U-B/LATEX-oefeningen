$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{2x^4-5x^3+x^2+6x-7}{x^2-3x+2}dx$\\

$\Rightarrow \dfrac{2x^4-5x^3+x^2+6x-7}{x^2-3x+2}
= 2x^2 + x + \dfrac{4x-7}{x^2-3x+2}$\\

$ = 2x^2+x+\dfrac{4x-7}{\left(x-1\right)\left(x-2\right)} $\\

$\dfrac{4x-7}{\left(x-1\right)\left(x-2\right)} = \dfrac{A}{x-1} + \dfrac{B}{x-2}$\\

$A = \left. \dfrac{4x-7}{x-2}\right|_{x=1}=\dfrac{-3}{-1}=3$\\

$B = \left. \dfrac{4x-7}{x-1}\right|_{x=2}=1$\\

$\Rightarrow \displaystyle\int \left(\dfrac{3}{x-1} + \dfrac{1}{x-2}\right)dx$\\


$= 3\ln{\left|x-1\right|}+\ln{\left|x-2\right|}+k$\\

$\boxed{\displaystyle\int \dfrac{2x^4-5x^3+x^2+6x-7}{x^2-3x+2}dx = \dfrac{2}{3}x^3+\dfrac{1}{2}x^2+3\ln{\left|x-1\right|}+\ln{\left|x-2\right|}+k}$\\
