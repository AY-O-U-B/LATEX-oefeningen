$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{\ln (\sin x)}{\cos^2 x}dx$\\

$\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
	u=\ln(\sin x) \\ 
	dv=\dfrac{1}{\cos^2 x}dx
\end{array}\right.
\Rightarrow 
\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
	du= 	\cot x\\
 	v= \tan x
\end{array}
\right.$\\

$= \tan x \ln(\sin x) - \displaystyle\int \tan x \cdot \cot x dx$\\

$= \tan x \ln(\sin x) - \displaystyle\int 1 dx$\\

$= \tan x \ln(\sin x) - x + C$\\
