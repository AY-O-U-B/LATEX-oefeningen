\normalsize

$\displaystyle \int_0^1\sqrt{1-e^{-2x}}\,dx$\\

Neem:\\

$u=e^{-x}$\\

$du=-e^{-x}\,dx$\\

$du=-u\,dx$\\

$dx=-\dfrac{du}{u}$\\

Grenzen aanpassen:\\

$x=0\Rightarrow u=1$\\

$x=1\Rightarrow u=e^{-1}$\\

Dus:\\

$\displaystyle \int_0^1\sqrt{1-e^{-2x}}\,dx$\\

$=
\displaystyle \int_1^{e^{-1}}
\sqrt{1-u^2}\left(-\dfrac{du}{u}\right)$\\

$=
\displaystyle \int_{e^{-1}}^1
\dfrac{\sqrt{1-u^2}}{u}\,du$\\

Neem:\\

$u=\cos t$\\

$du=-\sin t\,dt$\\

$\sqrt{1-u^2}=\sqrt{1-\cos^2t}$\\

$\sqrt{1-u^2}=\sin t$\\

Dan:\\

$\displaystyle \int\dfrac{\sqrt{1-u^2}}{u}\,du$\\

$=
\displaystyle \int
\dfrac{\sin t}{\cos t}(-\sin t)\,dt$\\

$=
-\displaystyle \int
\dfrac{\sin^2t}{\cos t}\,dt$\\

$=
-\displaystyle \int
\dfrac{1-\cos^2t}{\cos t}\,dt$\\

$=
-\displaystyle \int
\left(\dfrac{1}{\cos t}-\cos t\right)\,dt$\\

$=
-\displaystyle \int
(\sec t-\cos t)\,dt$\\

$=
-\ln|\sec t+\tan t|+\sin t$\\

Terug substitueren:\\

$\sin t=\sqrt{1-u^2}$\\

$\sec t=\dfrac{1}{u}$\\

$\tan t=\dfrac{\sqrt{1-u^2}}{u}$\\

$\sec t+\tan t
=
\dfrac{1+\sqrt{1-u^2}}{u}$\\

Dus:\\

$\displaystyle \int\dfrac{\sqrt{1-u^2}}{u}\,du$\\

$=
\sqrt{1-u^2}
-
\ln
\left|
\dfrac{1+\sqrt{1-u^2}}{u}
\right|$\\

Nu de grenzen invullen:\\

$\displaystyle \int_{e^{-1}}^1
\dfrac{\sqrt{1-u^2}}{u}\,du$\\

$=
\left[
\sqrt{1-u^2}
-
\ln
\left|
\dfrac{1+\sqrt{1-u^2}}{u}
\right|
\right]_{e^{-1}}^1$\\

$=
0
-
\left(
\sqrt{1-e^{-2}}
-
\ln
\left(
\dfrac{1+\sqrt{1-e^{-2}}}{e^{-1}}
\right)
\right)$\\

$=
-\sqrt{1-e^{-2}}
+
\ln
\left(
e(1+\sqrt{1-e^{-2}})
\right)$\\

$=
-\sqrt{1-e^{-2}}
+
\ln(e)
+
\ln(1+\sqrt{1-e^{-2}})$\\

$=
1+\ln(1+\sqrt{1-e^{-2}})
-\sqrt{1-e^{-2}}$\\

$=
\ln(e+\sqrt{e^2-1})
-
\dfrac{\sqrt{e^2-1}}{e}$\\

$\boxed{
\ln(e+\sqrt{e^2-1})
-
\dfrac{\sqrt{e^2-1}}{e}
}$\\
