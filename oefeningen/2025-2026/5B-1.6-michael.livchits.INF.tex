$I = \displaystyle\dint_{1}^{+\infty} \dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}dx$\\

Eerst vinden we de onbepaalde integraal\\

$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}dx$\\

Partiele integratie:\\

$\left\{
    \begin{array}{l}
        u =  \ln x \\
        dv = \dfrac{1}{x^2}dx
    \end{array}
\right .
\Rightarrow
\left\{
    \begin{array}{l}
        du = \dfrac{1}{x}dx \\
        v = -\dfrac{1}{x}
    \end{array}
\right .$\\

$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}dx = -\dfrac{\ln x}{x} + \displaystyle\int\dfrac{dx}{x^2} = -\dfrac{\ln x}{x} - \dfrac{1}{x}$\\

Neem de limiet\\

$I = \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow +\infty}\left[-\dfrac{\ln x}{x} - \dfrac{1}{x}\right]_{1}^{b}$\\

$= \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow +\infty}\left(-\dfrac{\ln b}{b} - \dfrac{1}{b} + 1\right)$\\

$= \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow +\infty}-\dfrac{\ln b}{b} +  \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow +\infty}\left(- \dfrac{1}{b} + 1\right)$\\

l'hopital toepassen op eerste limiet:\\

$= \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow +\infty}-\dfrac{1}{b} +  \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow +\infty}\left(- \dfrac{1}{b} + 1\right) = 1$\\

$\Rightarrow$ convergeert\\
