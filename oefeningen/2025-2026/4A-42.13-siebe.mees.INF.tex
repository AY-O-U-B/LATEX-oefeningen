$ \displaystyle\int \dfrac{4x+6}{x^2+4x+3}dx $\\

$ \Delta =4^2-4\cdot 3=4 \qquad x_{1,2}=\dfrac{-4\pm \sqrt{\Delta}}{2}, \qquad x_1=-1,\ x_2=-3 $\\

$ \displaystyle\int \dfrac{4x+6}{(x+1)(x+3)}dx =\dfrac{A}{x+1}+\dfrac{B}{x+3} $\\

Dan is \\
 
$ A=\dfrac{4x+6}{x+3}\bigg|_{x=-1}=1 $\\

$ B=\dfrac{4x+6}{x+1}\bigg|_{x=-3}=3 $\\

$ \Rightarrow \displaystyle\int \left( \dfrac{1}{x+1}+\dfrac{3}{x+3} \right)dx $\\

$ =\displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{x+1}dx+3\displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{x+3}dx $\\

$ =\ln|x+1|+3\ln|x+3|+C $\\
