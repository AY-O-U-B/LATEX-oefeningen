$\displaystyle \int x^3 \sqrt{x^2+1} dx$ \\

$$ t=x^2+1$$ $$dt=2xdx$$ $$\dfrac{dt}{2x} = dx$$ \\

$= \displaystyle \int \dfrac{x^3 \sqrt{t} dt}{2x}$ \\

$= \displaystyle \int \dfrac{x^2 \sqrt{t} dt}{2}$ \\

$= \displaystyle \int \dfrac{\left( t-1 \right) \sqrt{t} dt}{2}$ \\

$= \displaystyle \int \dfrac{\left( t-1 \right) t^{\tfrac{1}{2}} dt}{2}$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle \int \left( t^{\tfrac{3}{2}} - t^{\tfrac{1}{2}} \right)  dt$ \\

$= \left( \dfrac{t^{\tfrac{5}{2}}}{\tfrac{5}{2}} - \dfrac{t^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{\tfrac{3}{2}} \right) \dfrac{1}{2} + c$ \\

$= \left( \dfrac{2t^{\tfrac{5}{2}}}{5} - \dfrac{2t^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{3} \right) \dfrac{1}{2} + c$ \\

$= \dfrac{t^{\tfrac{5}{2}}}{5} - \dfrac{t^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{3} + c$ \\

$= \dfrac{\sqrt{t^5}}{5} - \dfrac{\sqrt{t^3}}{3} + c$ \\

$= \dfrac{\sqrt{\left( x^2+1 \right)^5}}{5} - \dfrac{\sqrt{\left( x^2+1 \right)^3}}{3} + c$ \\
