$\dint_{-1}^{0} \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow -1+} \dint_{a}^{0} \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$\\

Primitieve vinden:\\

$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} = \operatorname{Bgsin}{(x)}$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow -1+} \left(\operatorname{Bgsin}{(0)}-\operatorname{Bgsin}{(a)}\right)$\\

$= \left(0 + \dfrac{\pi}{2} \right) = \dfrac{\pi}{2}$\\

Integraal is convergent!\\
