$\dint \sin^{-2}x\cos^{-4}xdx$\\

$= \dint \dfrac{1}{\sin^2x\cos^2x}\cdot \dfrac{1}{\cos^2x}dx$\\

$t = \tan x \Rightarrow dt = \dfrac{1}{\cos^2x}dx$\\

$\Rightarrow \dfrac{1}{\cos^2x}= 1 + \tan^2x=1+t^2$ en $\dfrac{1}{\sin^2x}=1+\cot^2x=1+\dfrac{1}{t^2}$\\

$=\dint \dfrac{1}{(1+t^2)\left(\dfrac{t^2+1}{t^2}\right)}dt = \dint \dfrac{t^2}{t^4+2t^2+1}dt$\\

$=\dint (t^2+2+t^{-2})dt = \dfrac{1}{3}t^3+2t-\dfrac{1}{t}+C$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{3}\tan^3x+2\tan x - \dfrac{1}{\tan x}+ C$
