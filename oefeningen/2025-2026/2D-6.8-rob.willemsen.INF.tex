$f(x) = \dfrac{5x + 1}{9x^2 - 4}$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 0} \left(\dfrac{5x + 1}{9x^2 - 4}\right)$ \\

$= \dfrac{5 \cdot 0 + 1}{9\cdot(0)^2 - 4} = -\dfrac{1}{4}$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -1} \left(\dfrac{5x + 1}{9x^2 - 4}\right)$ \\

$= \dfrac{5 \cdot (-1) + 1}{9\cdot(-1)^2 - 4} = -\dfrac{4}{5}$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow 1}  \left(\dfrac{5x + 1}{9x^2 - 4}\right)$ \\

$= \dfrac{5 \cdot 1 + 1}{9\cdot1^2 - 4} = \dfrac{6}{5}$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -\tfrac{2}{3}}  \left(\dfrac{5x + 1}{9x^2 - 4}\right)$ \\

$= \dfrac{5 \cdot -\dfrac{2}{3} + 1}{9\cdot \left(-\dfrac{2}{3}\right)^2 - 4}$\\

$= \dfrac{-\dfrac{10}{3} + \dfrac{3}{3}}{\dfrac{36}{9} - 4} = \dfrac{-\dfrac{7}{3}}{0}$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -\tfrac{2}{3}-} f(x) = -\infty$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -\tfrac{2}{3}+} f(x) = +\infty$ \\
