$\displaystyle \int \dfrac{\sin2x}{1+\sin^2x}dx$\\

stel: $\sin^2x = t$\\

$\dfrac{dt}{dx} = (\sin^2x)'$\\

$\Leftrightarrow dx = \dfrac{dt}{2\sin x \cos x} $\\

$\displaystyle \int \dfrac{\sin2x}{1+\sin^2x}dx = \int \dfrac{dt }{1+t}$\\

$ = \ln |1+t|+c$\\

$ = \ln |1+\sin^2x|+c$
