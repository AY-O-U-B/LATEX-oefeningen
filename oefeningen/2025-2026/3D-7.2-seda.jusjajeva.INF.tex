$f(x) = e^x\sin x$ \\

$f'(x) = e^x\sin x + e^x\cos x = e^x\left(\sin x + \cos x\right)$ \\

$f'(x) = 0 \Leftrightarrow e^{x} = 0\vee\sin x + \cos x = 0$ \\

$e^x$: \\

$e^{x} = 0$ kan niet, $e^x$ raakt nooit y = 0. \\

$\sin x + \cos x$: \\

$\sin x + \cos x = 0$ \\

$\Leftrightarrow \sin x = -\cos x$ \\

$\Leftrightarrow \dfrac{\sin x}{\cos x} = -1$ \\

$\Leftrightarrow \tan x = -1$ \\

$\Leftrightarrow x = -\dfrac{\pi}{4} + k\pi$ \\

We checken waar de minima en maxima zitten: \\

$-\dfrac{\pi}{4}$: \\

$-\dfrac{\pi}{2} < -\dfrac{\pi}{4} \Rightarrow f'\left(-\dfrac{\pi}{2}\right) = -e^{-\tfrac{\pi}{2}} < 0$ \\

$0 > -\dfrac{\pi}{4} \Rightarrow f'(0) = 1 > 0$ \\

$\begin{array}{r|ccccccccc}
x     & -\infty &          & -\dfrac{\pi}{2} &          & -\dfrac{\pi}{4} &          & 0        &          & +\infty \\ 
\hline
f'(x) &         & -        & -               & -        & 0               & +        & +        & +        &\\ 
f(x)  &         & \searrow & \searrow        & \searrow & m               & \nearrow & \nearrow & \nearrow &\\
\end{array}$ \\

$x = -\dfrac{\pi}{4} + k2\pi$ zijn lokale minima \\

$\dfrac{3\pi}{4}$: \\

$0 < \dfrac{3\pi}{4} \Rightarrow f'(0) = 1 > 0$ \\

$\pi > \dfrac{3\pi}{4} \Rightarrow f'(\pi) = -e^{\pi} < 0$ \\

$\begin{array}{r|ccccccccc}
x     & -\infty &          & 0        &          & \dfrac{3\pi}{4} &          & \pi      &          & +\infty \\ 
\hline
f'(x) &         & +        & +        & +        & 0               & -        & -        & -        &\\ 
f(x)  &         & \nearrow & \nearrow & \nearrow & M               & \searrow & \searrow & \searrow &\\
\end{array}$ \\

$x = \dfrac{3\pi}{4} + k2\pi$ zijn lokale maxima \\
