$ \displaystyle\sum\limits_{n = 2}^\infty \dfrac{\ln n}{n^2} $\\

$ = \displaystyle\int_2^{+\infty}\dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}dx = -\displaystyle\int_2^{+\infty}\ln xd\left(\dfrac{1}{x}\right) $\\

$ = -\left[\dfrac{\ln x}{x}\right]_2^{+\infty} + \displaystyle\int_2^{+\infty}\dfrac{1}{x^2}dx $\\

$ = -\left[\dfrac{\ln x + 1}{x}\right]_2^{+\infty} $\\

$ = \lim\limits_{a \rightarrow +\infty} - \dfrac{-\ln a - 1}{a} - \dfrac{\ln 2 - 1}{2} = 0  - \dfrac{-\ln 2 - 1}{2} =  \dfrac{\ln 2 + 1}{2} \Rightarrow $ bestaat \\

$ \stackrel{\text{integraalcriterium}}{\Rightarrow} $ convergent \\
