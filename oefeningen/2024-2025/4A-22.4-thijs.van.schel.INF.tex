$\displaystyle\int x\sqrt{1-x^2}\Bgsin(x)dx$\\

$\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       u = \Bgsin(x)\\ 			
       dv = x\sqrt{1-x^2}dx
   \end{array}
\right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       du = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}dx\\			
       v = \dfrac{-(1-x^2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{3}
   \end{array}
\right.$

$= \dfrac{-(1-x^2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}\Bgsin(x)}{3} - \displaystyle\int \dfrac{-(1-x^2)dx}{3}$\\

$= \dfrac{-(1-x^2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}\Bgsin(x)}{3} - \left(\dfrac{1}{3} \displaystyle\int x^2dx - \dfrac{1}{3} \displaystyle\int 1dx\right)$\\

$= \dfrac{-(1-x^2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}\Bgsin(x)}{3} - \dfrac{x^3}{9} + \dfrac{x}{3}$ + C\\
