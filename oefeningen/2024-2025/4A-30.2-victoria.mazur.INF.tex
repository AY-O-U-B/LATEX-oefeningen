$\displaystyle\int x^3\sqrt{x^2 + 1}dx$ \\

$t = \sqrt{x^2 + 1}$ \\

$\sqrt{t^2 - 1} = x$ \\

$\dfrac{2t}{2\sqrt{t^2 - 1}}dt = dx$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\sqrt{t^2 - 1}^3 \cdot t}{\sqrt{t^2 - 1}}tdt$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\left(t^2 - 1\right)^{\tfrac{3}{2}} \cdot t^2}{\sqrt{t^2 - 1}}dt$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\left(t^2 - 1\right) \cdot \sqrt{t^2 - 1} \cdot t^2}{\sqrt{t^2 - 1}}dt$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \left(t^2 - 1\right)t^2dt$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \left(t^4 - t^2\right)dt$ \\

$= \dfrac{t^5}{5} - \dfrac{t^3}{3} + c$ \\

$= \dfrac{\left(\sqrt{x^2 + 1}\right)^5}{5} - \dfrac{\left(\sqrt{x^2 + 1}\right)^3}{3} + c$ \\
