$\displaystyle\int\dfrac{\ln(x)}{x^2}dx$ \\

$\left\{
	\begin{array}{l}
		u = \ln x\\
		dv = \dfrac{dx}{x^2}
	\end{array}
\right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
	\begin{array}{l}
		du = \dfrac{1}{x}dx\\
		v = -\dfrac{1}{x}
	\end{array}
\right.$

$-\dfrac{\ln(x)}{x} + \displaystyle\int\dfrac{dx}{x^2}$\\

$= \dfrac{-\ln(x)}{x}-\dfrac{1}{x}$ + C\\
