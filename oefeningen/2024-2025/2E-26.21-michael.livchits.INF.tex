$2\log_4{x}+\dfrac{1}{\log_{x-4}{2}} = 2+\dfrac{1}{\log_{x-3}{\sqrt{2}}}$\\

$\Leftrightarrow2\dfrac{\log_2{x}}{\log_2{4}}+\log_{2}\left({x-4}\right) = 2+\dfrac{1}{2}\log_{\sqrt{2}}{x-3}$\\

$\Leftrightarrow2\dfrac{\log_2{x}}{\log_2{4}}+\log_{2}\left({x-4}\right) = 2+\dfrac{\log_2\left({x-3}\right)}{2\log_{2}{\sqrt{2}}}$\\

$\Leftrightarrow\log_2{x}+\log_{2}\left({x-4}\right) = \log_2{4}+\log_2\left({x-3}\right)$\\

$\Leftrightarrow\log_2\left({x\left(x-4\right)}\right)= \log_2\left({4\left(x-3\right)}\right)$\\

$\Leftrightarrow x(x-4) = 4(x-3) $\\

$\Leftrightarrow x^2 - 4x = 4x - 12 $\\

$\Leftrightarrow x^2 - 8x + 12 = 0 $\\
