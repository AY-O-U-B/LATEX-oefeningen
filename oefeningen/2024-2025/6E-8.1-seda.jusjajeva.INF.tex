$\sin x = T_n(\sin x,0)(x) + R_n(x)$ \\

$R_n(x) = \dfrac{(x - a)^{n + 1}}{(n + 1)!}f^{(n + 1)}(c)$ \\

Stel $-|x| < c < |x|$: \\

$R_{2n + 1}(x) = \dfrac{x^{2n + 2}\sin^{(2n + 2)}c}{(2n + 2)!}$ \\

$\overset{x = -0.7}\Rightarrow |R_{2n + 1}(-0.7)| = \dfrac{0.7^{2n + 2}\sin^{(2n + 2)}c}{(2n + 2)!}$ \\

$\overset{|\sin^{(k)}c| \le 1}\Rightarrow |R_{2n+1}(-0.7)| \le \dfrac{0.7^{2n+2}}{(2n+2)!}$ \\

$\overset{n = 4}\Rightarrow |R_{9}(-0.7)| \le \dfrac{0.7^{10}}{10!}$ \\

$\dfrac{0.7^{10}}{10!} \approx 7.78 \cdot 10^{-9} < 5 \cdot 10^{-8}$ \\

We nemen dus de Taylorpolynoom tot en met graad 9:

$T_n(\sin x,0)x = \displaystyle\sum_{k = 0}^{n} \dfrac{(\sin 0)^{(k)}}{k!}x^k$ \\

$= \dfrac{\sin 0}{1}x^0 + \dfrac{\cos 0}{1}x + \dfrac{-\sin 0}{2}x^2 + \dfrac{-\cos 0}{6}x^3 + \dfrac{\sin 0}{24}x^4 + \dfrac{\cos 0}{120}x^5 + \dfrac{-\sin 0}{720}x^6 + \dfrac{-\cos 0}{5040}x^7 + \dfrac{\sin 0}{40320}x^8 + \dfrac{\cos 0}{362880}x^9 ...$ \\

$= x + -\dfrac{x^3}{6} + \dfrac{x^5}{120} - \dfrac{x^7}{5040} + \dfrac{x^9}{362880} ...$ \\

$\sin(-0.7) \approx -0.7 + -\dfrac{(-0.7)^3}{6} + \dfrac{(-0.7)^5}{120} - \dfrac{(-0.7)^7}{5040} + \dfrac{(-0.7)^9}{362880}$ \\

$\Rightarrow \sin(-0.7) \approx -0.6442177$ \\
