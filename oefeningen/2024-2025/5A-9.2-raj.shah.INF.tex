$\displaystyle\int_{1}^{2} (x^2 + 4)^\tfrac{1}{3}\cdot x dx$\\

Stel $x^2 + 4 = t \Rightarrow dt = 2xdx \Leftrightarrow dx = \dfrac{dt}{2x}$\\

$x_1 = 5$ en $x_2 = 8$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2} \cdot \displaystyle\int_{5}^{8} t^{\tfrac{1}{3}} dt$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2} \cdot \left[\dfrac{t^{\tfrac{4}{3}}}{{\dfrac{4}{3}}}\right]_{5}^{8}$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2} \cdot \left(\left(\dfrac{3\cdot 8^{\tfrac{4}{3}}}{4}\right) - \left(\dfrac{3\cdot 5^{\tfrac{4}{3}}}{4}\right)\right)$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2} \cdot \left(\dfrac{24\sqrt[3]{8}}{4} - \dfrac{15\sqrt[3]{5}}{4}\right)$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2} \cdot \left(\dfrac{48}{4} - \dfrac{15\sqrt[3]{5}}{4}\right)$

$ = 6 - \dfrac{15\sqrt[3]{5}}{8}$
