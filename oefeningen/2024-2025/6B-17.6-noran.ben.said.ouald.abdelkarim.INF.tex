$1^2 \cdot 4 + 2^2 \cdot 5 + \dots + n^2(n + 3)$ \\

$= s_3(n) + 3 \cdot s_2(n)$ \\

waarbij: \\

$s_3(n) = \displaystyle\sum\limits_{k=1}^{n} k^2 \cdot k = \displaystyle\sum\limits_{k=1}^{n} k^3 = \left( \dfrac{n(n+1)}{2} \right)^2 = \dfrac{n^2(n+1)^2}{4}$ \\

$s_2(n) = \displaystyle\sum\limits_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \dfrac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$ \\

Dus: \\

$= \dfrac{n^2(n+1)^2}{4} + 3 \cdot \dfrac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$ \\

$= \dfrac{n^4}{4} + \dfrac{n^3}{2} + \dfrac{n^2}{4} + 3 \cdot \left( \dfrac{n^3}{3} + \dfrac{n^2}{2} + \dfrac{n}{6} \right)$ \\

$= \dfrac{n^4}{4} + \dfrac{3 \cdot n^3}{2} + \dfrac{7 \cdot n^2}{4} + \dfrac{n}{2}$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{4}n(n+1)(n^2 + 5n + 2)$ \\

Omdat $n$ of $n+1$ even is en $n^2 + 5n + 2$ altijd even is, is de teller dus deelbaar door $4$. \\
