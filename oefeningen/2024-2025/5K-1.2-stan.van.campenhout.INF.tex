$ r = \theta^2 $\\

$ r' = 2\theta \Rightarrow \left(r'\right)^2 = 4\theta^2 $\\

$ r^2 = \theta^4 $\\

$ \Rightarrow\displaystyle\int_0^{4\sqrt{2}}\sqrt{\theta^4+4\theta^2}d\theta = \displaystyle\int_0^{4\sqrt{2}}\theta\sqrt{\theta^2+4}d\theta 
= \dfrac{1}{2}\displaystyle\int_0^{4\sqrt{2}}\sqrt{\theta^2+4}d\left(\theta^2+4\right) $\\

$ = \left[\dfrac{\left(\theta^2+4\right)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{3}\right]_0^{4\sqrt{2}} = \dfrac{6^3}{3} - \dfrac{2^3}{3} = \dfrac{208}{3} $\\
