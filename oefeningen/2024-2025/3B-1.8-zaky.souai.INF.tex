$f(x) = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x-5}}$ \\

$\Rightarrow f(x) = (\sqrt{x-5})^{\tfrac{-1}{2}} $ \\

$\Rightarrow f'(x) = \dfrac{-1}{2} \cdot (\sqrt{x-5})^{\tfrac{-3}{2}}$ \\

$\Rightarrow f''(x) = \dfrac{3}{4} \cdot (\sqrt{x-5})^{\tfrac{-5}{2}}$ \\

$\Rightarrow f''(x) = \dfrac{3}{4 \cdot \sqrt{(x-5)^5}}$ \\
