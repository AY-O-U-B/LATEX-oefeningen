$ f(x) = \ln\left(x^2 + \sqrt{x^4 - 1}\right) $\\

$ f'(x) = \dfrac{1}{x^2 + \sqrt{x^4 - 1}} \cdot \left(2x + \dfrac{1}{2 \sqrt{x^4 - 1}} \cdot 4x^3 \right) $\\

$ =  \dfrac{2x + \dfrac{2x^3}{\sqrt{x^4 - 1}}}{x^2 + \sqrt{x^4 - 1}} $\\

$ = \dfrac{\dfrac{2x\sqrt{x^4 - 1} + 2x^3}{\sqrt{x^4 - 1}}}{x^2 + \sqrt{x^4 - 1}} $\\

$ = \dfrac{2x\left(\sqrt{x^4 - 1} + x^2\right)}{\sqrt{x^4 - 1}\left(x^2 + \sqrt{x^4 - 1}\right)} $\\

$ = \dfrac{2x}{\sqrt{x^4 - 1}} $\\
