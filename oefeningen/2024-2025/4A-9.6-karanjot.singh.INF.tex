$\displaystyle\int \sin(x) \tan^2(x) dx$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \sin(x) (\sec^2(x) - 1) dx$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \sin(x) \sec^2(x) dx - \displaystyle\int \sin(x) dx$ \\

Voor de eerste integraal gebruiken we substitutie. \\

Laat $u = \cos(x)$, dan $du = -\sin(x) dx$ \\

$\displaystyle\int \sin(x) \sec^2(x) dx = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\sin(x)}{\cos^2(x)} dx$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{-du}{u^2} = -\displaystyle\int u^{-2} du$ \\

$= -\left[ -\dfrac{1}{u} \right] + C = \dfrac{1}{\cos(x)} + C = \sec(x) + C$ \\

Voor de tweede integraal: \\

$\displaystyle\int \sin(x) dx = -\cos(x) + C$ \\

Dus: \\

$\displaystyle\int \sin(x) \tan^2(x) dx = \sec(x) + \cos(x) + C$ \\
