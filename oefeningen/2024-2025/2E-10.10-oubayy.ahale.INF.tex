$ 2^{2x+1} - 9 \cdot 2^{x-1} + 1 = 0 $\\

Substitueer $ 2^x = y $\\

$ \Rightarrow y^2 \cdot 2 - 9 \cdot \dfrac{y}{2} + 1 = 0 $\\

$ D = b^2 - 4ac $\\

$ = \dfrac{81}{4} - 4 \cdot 2 \cdot 1 $\\

$ = \dfrac{81}{4} - 8 \cdot \dfrac{4}{4} $\\

$ = \dfrac{81 - 32}{4} $\\

$ = \dfrac{49}{4} $\\

$ y_1 = \dfrac{-b - \sqrt{D}}{2a} = \dfrac{\dfrac{9}{2} - \dfrac{7}{2}}{4} = \dfrac{1}{4} $\\

$ y_2 = \dfrac{-b + \sqrt{D}}{2a} = \dfrac{\dfrac{9}{2} + \dfrac{7}{2}}{4} = \dfrac{8}{4} = 2 $\\

Omzetten naar $ x $: \\

$ y = \dfrac{1}{4} \Rightarrow 2^x = \dfrac{1}{4} $\\

$ \Rightarrow 2^x = \dfrac{1}{2^2} $\\

$ \Rightarrow x = -2 $\\

$ y = 2 \Rightarrow 2^x = 2 $\\

$ \Rightarrow x = 1 $\\

$ V = \{ -2, 1 \} $\\
