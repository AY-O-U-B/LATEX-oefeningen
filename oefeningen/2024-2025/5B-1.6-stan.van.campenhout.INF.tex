$ \displaystyle\int\limits_1^{+\infty}\dfrac{\ln(x)}{x^2} dx = -\displaystyle\int\limits_1^{+\infty}\ln(x)d\left(\dfrac{1}{x}\right)
= \lim\limits_{a\rightarrow+\infty}-\displaystyle\int\limits_1^a\ln(x)d\left(\dfrac{1}{x}\right) $\\

$ = \lim\limits_{a\rightarrow+\infty}\left[\dfrac{\ln(x)}{x}\right]_1^a - \lim\limits_{a\rightarrow+\infty}\left[\dfrac{1}{x}\right]_1^a = -0 + 0 - 0 +1 = 1 $\\
