Bereken de volgende bepaalde integralen door partiële integratie\\

$\displaystyle\displaystyle\int_{0}^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}} x^{2} \sin x \cos^{3} x \cdot dx$ \\

onbepaalde integraal: \\

$ \left\{ \begin{array}{l} 
    u = x^{2} \\
    
    dv = \sin x \cos^{3} x \cdot dx
\end{array} \right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} 
    du = 2x \cdot dx\\
    
    v = -\dfrac{1}{4} \cos^{4} x\\
\end{array} \right. $ \\

$I = -\dfrac{1}{4} x^{2} \cos^{4} x + \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int x \cos^{4} x \cdot dx$ \\

Dubbel toepassen van de halveringsformule geeft:\\

$\displaystyle\int x \cos^{4} x \cdot dx = \displaystyle\int x \left( \dfrac{3}{8} + \dfrac{1}{2} \cos(2x) + \dfrac{1}{8} \cos(4x) \right) \cdot dx$ \\

$= \dfrac{3}{16} x^{2} + \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int x \cos(2x) \cdot dx + \dfrac{1}{8} \displaystyle\int x \cos(4x) \cdot dx$ \\

De termen $\displaystyle\int x \cos(ax) \cdot dx$: \\

$\displaystyle\int x \cos(ax) \cdot dx = \dfrac{x}{a} \sin(ax) + \dfrac{1}{a^{2}} \cos(ax)$ \\

$I = -\dfrac{1}{4} x^{2} \cos^{4} x + \dfrac{3}{32} x^{2} + \dfrac{x}{8} \sin(2x) + \dfrac{1}{16} \cos(2x) + \dfrac{x}{64} \sin(4x) + \dfrac{1}{256} \cos(4x) + c$ \\

De bepaalde integraal: \\

$I = \left[ -\dfrac{1}{4} x^{2} \cos^{4} x + \dfrac{3}{32} x^{2} + \dfrac{x}{8} \sin(2x) + \dfrac{1}{16} \cos(2x) + \dfrac{x}{64} \sin(4x) + \dfrac{1}{256} \cos(4x) \right]_{0}^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}}$ \\

bovengrens: $\dfrac{\pi}{2}$: \\

$\left( 0 + \dfrac{3\pi^{2}}{128} + 0 - \dfrac{1}{16} + 0 + \dfrac{1}{256} \right) = \dfrac{3\pi^{2}}{128} - \dfrac{15}{256}$ \\

ondergrens: $0$: \\

$\left( 0 + 0 + 0 + \dfrac{1}{16} + 0 + \dfrac{1}{256} \right) = \dfrac{17}{256}$ \\

$I = \left( \dfrac{3\pi^{2}}{128} - \dfrac{15}{256} \right) - \dfrac{17}{256} = \dfrac{3\pi^{2}}{128} - \dfrac{32}{256} = \dfrac{3\pi^{2}}{128} - \dfrac{1}{8}$ \\
