$ \SUM \dfrac{1}{n \cdot \ln\left( 1 + \dfrac{1}{n}\right)} = \SUM \dfrac{1}{\ln\left( 1 + \dfrac{1}{n}\right)^n} $ \\

negatief criterium:\\

$ \LIM \dfrac{1}{\ln\left( 1 + \dfrac{1}{n}\right)^n} $ \\

$ = \dfrac{1}{\LIM \ln\left( 1 + \dfrac{1}{n}\right)^n}$ \\

$ = \dfrac{1}{\ln\left(\LIM \left(1 + \dfrac{1}{n}\right)^n\right)} $ \\

$ = \dfrac{1}{\ln(e)} = 1 \neq 0 $ \\

$ \Rightarrow \SUM \dfrac{1}{n \cdot \ln\left( 1 + \dfrac{1}{n}\right)}$ is divergent \\
