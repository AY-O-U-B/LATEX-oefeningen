$f(x,y) = x^3 + x^2y^3-3y^4$ in $\left(1,-1\right)$, graad 2\\

$f(1,-1) = -3$\\

$\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x}\left(x,y\right) = 3x^2+2xy^3 \Rightarrow \dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x}\left(1,-1\right) = 1$\\

$\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}\left(x,y\right) = 3x^2y^2-12y^3 \Rightarrow \dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}\left(1,-1\right) = 15$\\

$\dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}\left(x,y\right) = 6x+3y^3 \Rightarrow \dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}\left(1,-1\right) = 4$\\

$\dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2}\left(x,y\right) = 6x^y-36y^2 \Rightarrow \dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2}\left(1,-1\right) = -42$\\

$\dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x \partial y}\left(x,y\right) = \dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y \partial x}\left(x,y\right) = 6xy^2 \Rightarrow \dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x \partial y}\left(1,-1\right) = \dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y \partial x}\left(1,-1\right) = 6$\\
