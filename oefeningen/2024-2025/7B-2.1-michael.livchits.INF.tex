$w = \ln(x^2+y^2+z^2)$ met $x = s-t$, $y = s+t$ en $z = 2\sqrt{st}$\\

$\dfrac{\partial w}{\partial x} = \dfrac{2x}{x^2+y^2+z^2}$\\

$\dfrac{\partial w}{\partial y} = \dfrac{2y}{x^2+y^2+z^2}$\\

$\dfrac{\partial w}{\partial z} = \dfrac{2z}{x^2+y^2+z^2}$\\

Eerst voor s:\\

$\dfrac{\partial x}{\partial s} = 1$\\

$\dfrac{\partial y}{\partial s} = 1$\\

$\dfrac{\partial z}{\partial s} = \dfrac{t}{\sqrt{st}}$\\

$Dw(x,y,z)=\left(\begin{array}{ccc}
\dfrac{2x}{x^2+y^2+z^2} & \dfrac{2y}{x^2+y^2+z^2} &\dfrac{2z}{x^2+y^2+z^2}
\end{array}\right)$\\

$D(x,y,z)(s)=\left(\begin{array}{c}
1 \\
1 \\
\dfrac{t}{\sqrt{st}}
\end{array}\right)$\\

$Dw(x,y,z)\cdot D(x,y,z)(s) = \dfrac{2x+2y+\dfrac{2zt}{\sqrt{st}}}{x^2+y^2+z^2}$\\

$= \dfrac{2(s-t)+2(s+t)+4t}{(s-t)^2+(s+t)^2+4st} = \dfrac{2}{(s+t)}$\\

$\dfrac{\partial w}{\partial s}= \dfrac{2}{(s+t)}$\\

Nu voor $t$:\\

$\dfrac{\partial x}{\partial t} = -1$\\

$\dfrac{\partial y}{\partial t} = 1$\\

$\dfrac{\partial z}{\partial t} = \dfrac{s}{\sqrt{st}}$\\

$D(x,y,z)(t)=\left(\begin{array}{c}
-1 \\
1 \\
\dfrac{s}{\sqrt{st}}
\end{array}\right)$\\

$Dw(x,y,z)\cdot D(x,y,z)(t) = \dfrac{-2x+2y+\dfrac{2zs}{\sqrt{st}}}{x^2+y^2+z^2}$\\

$= \dfrac{-2(s-t)+2(s+t)+4s}{2(s+t)^2} = \dfrac{2}{s+t}$\\

$\dfrac{\partial w}{\partial t} = \dfrac{2}{s+t}$\\
