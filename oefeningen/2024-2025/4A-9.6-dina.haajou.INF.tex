$ \displaystyle\int \sin(x) \tan^2(x) dx $ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \sin(x)(\sec^2(x) - 1) dx $ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\sin(x)}{\cos^2(x)} dx - \displaystyle\int \sin(x) dx $ \\

Substitueer $u = \cos(x)$ \\

$du = -\sin(x) dx$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{-du}{u^2} - (-\cos(x)) + C $ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{\cos(x)} + \cos(x) + C $ \\

$= \sec(x) + \cos(x) + C$ \\
