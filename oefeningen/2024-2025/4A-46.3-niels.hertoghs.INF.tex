$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{3x^2+6}{x^4+5x^2+4} dx = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{3x^2+6}{(x^2+1)(x^2+4)} dx$\\

we splitsen eerst de partieelbreuken:\\

$\dfrac{3x^2+6}{(x^2+1)(x^2+4)} = \dfrac{Ax+B}{x^2+1} + \dfrac{Cx+D}{x^2+4} $\\

$A(i) + B = \dfrac{3x^2+6}{x^2+4}  \bigg|_{x = i} = 1 $ dit impliceert dat: $A = 0$ en $B =1$ \\

$C(2i) + D =  \dfrac{3x^2+6}{x^2+1} \bigg|_{x = 2i} = 2 $  dit impliceert dat: $C=0$ en $D =2$\\

$I = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{x^2+1}dx + 2\displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{x^2+4}dx$\\

$= \operatorname{Bgtan}(x) + \operatorname{Bgtan}\left(\dfrac{x}{2}\right) + c $\\
