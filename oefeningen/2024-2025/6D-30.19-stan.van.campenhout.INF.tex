$ \displaystyle\sum\limits_{n = 1}^\infty \dfrac{\left(-1\right)^n\left(n+1\right)}{n^2} = \displaystyle\sum\limits_{n = 1}^\infty \left(-1\right)^n\cdot\dfrac{n+1}{n^2} $\\

$ \left(-1\right)^n = -1, 1 -1, 1, ... \Rightarrow $ partieelsommen = $ \{-1, 0\} \rightarrow $ begrensd \\

$ \lim\limits_{n \rightarrow +\infty}\dfrac{n + 1}{n^2} = 0 $\\

$ \stackrel{\text{Dirichelet}}{\Rightarrow} $ convergent \\
