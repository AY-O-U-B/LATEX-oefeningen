$\displaystyle \int_0^1 \dfrac{e^{2x}}{\sqrt{e^x + 1}} dx$ \\

$t = e^x + 1 \Rightarrow dt = e^x dx$ \\

$e^x = t - 1 \Rightarrow e^{2x} = (t - 1)^2$ \\

$x = 0 \Rightarrow t = 2$ \\

$x = 1 \Rightarrow t = e + 1$ \\

$\displaystyle \int_0^1 \dfrac{e^{2x}}{\sqrt{e^x + 1}} dx = \displaystyle \int_2^{e+1} \dfrac{(t - 1)^2}{\sqrt{t}(t - 1)} dt$ \\

$= \displaystyle \int_2^{e+1} \dfrac{t - 1}{\sqrt{t}} dt$ \\

$= \left[ \dfrac{2\sqrt{t}(t - 3)}{3} \right]_2^{e+1}$ \\

$= \dfrac{(2e - 4)\sqrt{e + 1} - 2\sqrt{2}}{3}$ \\
