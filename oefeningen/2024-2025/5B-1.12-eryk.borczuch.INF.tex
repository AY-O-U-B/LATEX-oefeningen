$\displaystyle\int_{-\infty}^{0} xe^x dx$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \to -\infty} \displaystyle\int_{a}^{0} x e^x $\\

$\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       u = x\\
       dv = e^x dx
   \end{array}
\right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       du = dx \\
       v =e^x
   \end{array}
\right.$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \to -\infty} \left(x e^x - \displaystyle\int_{a}^{0}e^x dx\right) $\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \to -\infty} [xe^x - e^x]_{a}^{0}$\\

$= \lim\limits_{a \to -\infty} (0e^0-e^0)-(ae^a-e^a)$\\

$= -1 +\infty\cdot e^{-\infty} + e^{-\infty} $\\

$= -1 $\\
