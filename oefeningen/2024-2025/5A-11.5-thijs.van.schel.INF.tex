$\bdint e^{2x} \cos(3x)dx$\\

$\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       u = \cos(3x)\\ 
			
       dv =  e^{2x}dx
   \end{array}
\right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       du = -3\sin(3x)dx \\
			
       v =  \dfrac{1}{2}e^{2x}
   \end{array}
\right.$\\

O.I.:\\

$I = \dfrac{1}{2}e^{2x}\cos(3x) + \dfrac{3}{2}\dint e^{2x}\sin(3x)dx$\\

$\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       u = \sin(3x)\\ 
			
       dv =  e^{2x}dx
   \end{array}
\right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       du = 3\cos(3x)dx \\
			
       v =  \dfrac{1}{2}e^{2x}
   \end{array}
\right.$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2}e^{2x}\cos(3x) + \dfrac{3}{4}e^{2x}\sin(3x) - \dfrac{9}{4}\dint e^{2x}\cos(3x)dx$\\

$I = \dfrac{1}{2}e^{2x}\cos(3x) + \dfrac{3}{4}e^{2x}\sin(3x) - \dfrac{9}{4}I$\\

$I + \dfrac{9}{4} = \dfrac{1}{2}e^{2x}\cos(3x) + \dfrac{3}{4}e^{2x}\sin(3x)+c$\\

$\dfrac{13}{4}I = e^{2x}\left(\dfrac{1}{2}\cos(3x) + \dfrac{3}{4}\sin(3x)\right)+c$\\

$I = \dfrac{4}{13} e^{2x}\left(\dfrac{1}{2}\cos(3x) + \dfrac{3}{4}\sin(3x)\right)+c$\\

B.I.:\\

$\dfrac{4}{13}e^\pi\left(\dfrac{1}{2}\cdot 0 + \dfrac{3}{4} \cdot (-1)\right) - \dfrac{4}{13} \cdot 1 \left(\dfrac{1}{2} \cdot 1 + 0\right)$\\

$ = \dfrac{-3e^\pi}{13} - \dfrac{2}{13}$\\

$ = \dfrac{-3e^\pi - 2}{13}$
