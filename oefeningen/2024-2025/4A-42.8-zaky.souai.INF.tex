$\displaystyle 42.8\quad \int \dfrac{dx}{x^3-7x+6}$\\

$\displaystyle x^3-7x+6=(x-1)(x-2)(x+3)$\\

$\displaystyle \dfrac{1}{x^3-7x+6}=\dfrac{1}{(x-1)(x-2)(x+3)}=\dfrac{A}{x-1}+\dfrac{B}{x-2}+\dfrac{C}{x+3}$\\

$\displaystyle A=\left.\dfrac{1}{(x-2)(x+3)}\right|_{x=1}=\dfrac{1}{(-1)(4)}=-\dfrac{1}{4}$\\

$\displaystyle B=\left.\dfrac{1}{(x-1)(x+3)}\right|_{x=2}=\dfrac{1}{(1)(5)}=\dfrac{1}{5}$\\

$\displaystyle C=\left.\dfrac{1}{(x-1)(x-2)}\right|_{x=-3}=\dfrac{1}{(-4)(-5)}=\dfrac{1}{20}$\\

$\displaystyle \dfrac{1}{x^3-7x+6}=-\dfrac{1}{4(x-1)}+\dfrac{1}{5(x-2)}+\dfrac{1}{20(x+3)}$\\

$\displaystyle \int \dfrac{dx}{x^3-7x+6}=-\dfrac{1}{4}\ln|x-1|+\dfrac{1}{5}\ln|x-2|+\dfrac{1}{20}\ln|x+3|+c$
