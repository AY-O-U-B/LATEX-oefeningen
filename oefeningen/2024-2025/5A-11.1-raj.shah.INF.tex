$\displaystyle\int_{0}^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}} x^2 \sin x dx $\\

$\left\{
    \begin{array}{l}
        u = x^2 \\
        dv = \sin x dx
    \end{array}
\right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
    \begin{array}{l}
        du =  2x dx \\
        v = - \cos x
    \end{array}
\right.$\\

$ \overset{PI}{=} [-x^2 \cos x]_{0}^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}} + \displaystyle\int_{0}^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}} \cos x \cdot 2x dx$\\

$ = \left(-\dfrac{\pi}{4} \cdot 0\right)+ 2\displaystyle\int_{0}^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}} x \cos x dx$

$\left\{
    \begin{array}{l}
        u = x \\
        dv = \cos x dx
    \end{array}
\right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
    \begin{array}{l}
        du =  dx \\
        v = \sin x
    \end{array}
\right.$\\

$ \overset{PI}{=} 2\cdot \left([x \sin x]_{0}^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}} - \displaystyle\int_{0}^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}}\sin x dx\right) $\\

$ = 2\left(\left(\dfrac{\pi}{2} \cdot 1\right) + [\cos x]_0^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}}\right) = 2\left(\dfrac{\pi}{2} + (0-1)\right)$\\

$ = \pi - 2$\\
