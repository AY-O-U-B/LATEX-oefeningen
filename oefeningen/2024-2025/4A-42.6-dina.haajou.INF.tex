$I = \bigint \dfrac{x}{2x^2 - 3x + 1} dx$ \\

$= \bigint \dfrac{x}{(2x - 1)(x - 1)} dx$ \\

We gebruiken de regel van Fuss: \\

$A = \left. \left( \dfrac{x}{x - 1} \right) \right|_{x = \tfrac{1}{2}} = -1$ \\

$B = \left. \left( \dfrac{x}{2x - 1} \right) \right|_{x = 1} = 1$ \\

$\Rightarrow \dfrac{x}{(2x - 1)(x - 1)} = \dfrac{-1}{2x - 1} + \dfrac{1}{x - 1}$ \\

$I = \bigint \left( \dfrac{1}{x - 1} - \dfrac{1}{2x - 1} \right) dx$ \\

$t = 2x - 1 \Rightarrow \dfrac{1}{2} dt = dx$ \\

$I = \ln |x - 1| - \dfrac{1}{2} \bigint \dfrac{1}{t} dt$ \\

$= \ln |x - 1| - \dfrac{1}{2} \ln |2x - 1| + c$ \\
