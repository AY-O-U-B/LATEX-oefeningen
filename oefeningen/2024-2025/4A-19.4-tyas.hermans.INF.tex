$\displaystyle\int (e^{-3x} \cos x) dx$\\

$
\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
u = \cos x \\
dv = e^{-3x} dx 
\end{array}
\right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
du = (-\sin x)dx \\
v = \dfrac{e^{-3x}}{-3}
\end{array}
\right.
$\\

$I = \dfrac{e^{-3x} \cos x}{-3} - \dfrac{1}{3}\displaystyle\int e^x(-\sin x)dx$\\

$
\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
u = \sin x \\
dv = e^{-3x} dx 
\end{array}
\right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
du = (\cos x)dx \\
v = \dfrac{e^{-3x}}{-3}
\end{array}
\right.
$\\

$I = \dfrac{e^{-3x} \cos x}{-3}+\dfrac{e^{-3x}\sin x}{9} -\dfrac{1}{9}I$\\

$\Rightarrow \dfrac{10}{9}I =  \dfrac{e^{-3x} \cos x}{-3}+\dfrac{e^{-3x}\sin x}{9} + C$\\

$\Rightarrow I = \dfrac{e^{-3x}\sin x - 3e^{-3x}\cos x}{10} + C$
