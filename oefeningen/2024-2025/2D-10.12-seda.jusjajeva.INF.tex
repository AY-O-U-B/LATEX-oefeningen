$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -\tfrac{1}{2}} \dfrac{-6x^2-x+1}{-10x^2-x+2}$ \\

$-6x^2 - x + 1 = -6\left(x+\dfrac{1}{2}\right)\left(x-\dfrac{1}{3}\right)$ \\

$-10x^2 - x + 2 = -10\left(x+\dfrac{1}{2}\right)\left(x-\dfrac{2}{5}\right)$

$\lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -\tfrac{1}{2}} \dfrac{-6x^2-x+1}{-10x^2-x+2} = \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -\tfrac{1}{2}} \dfrac{-6\left(x+\dfrac{1}{2}\right)\left(x-\dfrac{1}{3}\right)}{-10\left(x+\dfrac{1}{2}\right)\left(x-\dfrac{2}{5}\right)} $ \\

$= \lim\limits_{x \rightarrow -\tfrac{1}{2}} \dfrac{-3x+1}{-5x+2} = \dfrac{5}{9}$ \\
