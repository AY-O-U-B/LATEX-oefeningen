Bereken $\cos\left(\dfrac13\right)$ tot op 12 decimalen door middel van een Taylorreeks. Geef ook de restterm.

\begin{enumerate}

\item Taylorontwikkeling van $\cos x$ rond $0$\\

$f(x)=\cos x$\\

$f'(x)=-\sin x$\\

$f''(x)=-\cos x$\\

$f'''(x)=\sin x$\\

$f^{iv}(x)=\cos x$\\

$f(0)=1$\\

$f'(0)=0$\\

$f''(0)=-1$\\

$f'''(0)=0$\\

$f^{iv}(0)=1$\\

$\Rightarrow f^{(2k+1)}(0)=0$ en $f^{(2k)}(0)=(-1)^k$\\

$\Rightarrow T_n\left(\cos x,0\right)(x)=\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\dfrac{f^{(k)}(0)}{k!}x^k$\\

Alleen even termen blijven over:\\

$=\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\dfrac{f^{(2k)}(0)}{(2k)!}x^{2k}
=\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\dfrac{(-1)^k}{(2k)!}x^{2k}$\\

Dus

\[
\cos x
=
1-\dfrac{x^2}{2!}
+\dfrac{x^4}{4!}
-\dfrac{x^6}{6!}
+\dfrac{x^8}{8!}
-\dfrac{x^{10}}{10!}
+R_{10}(x)
\]

Stel nu $x=\dfrac13$, dan hebben we dus

\begin{align*}
\cos\left(\dfrac13\right)
&=
1-\dfrac{\left(\dfrac13\right)^2}{2!}
+\dfrac{\left(\dfrac13\right)^4}{4!}
-\dfrac{\left(\dfrac13\right)^6}{6!}
+\dfrac{\left(\dfrac13\right)^8}{8!}
-\dfrac{\left(\dfrac13\right)^{10}}{10!}
+R_{10}\left(\dfrac13\right)
\\[2mm]
&\approx
0.944956946314085
+
R_{10}\left(\dfrac13\right)
\end{align*}

\item Restterm\\

$R_{10}\left(\dfrac13\right)
=
\dfrac{f^{(12)}(\xi)}{12!}\left(\dfrac13\right)^{12}
\quad \xi\in\left[0,\dfrac13\right]$\\

$\left|f^{(12)}(\xi)\right|\leq1$\\

$\Rightarrow
\left|R_{10}\left(\dfrac13\right)\right|
\leq
\dfrac{\left(\dfrac13\right)^{12}}{12!}$\\

$\Rightarrow
\left|R_{10}\left(\dfrac13\right)\right|
=
\dfrac{1}{3^{12}\cdot12!}
\approx
3.928206751\cdot10^{-15}$\\

Aangezien

\[
3.928206751\cdot10^{-15}<10^{-12}
\]

mogen we afronden tot op 12 decimalen. Bijgevolg is

\[
\cos\left(\dfrac13\right)\approx0.944956946315
\]

\end{enumerate}
