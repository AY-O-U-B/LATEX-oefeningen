$T_5(f,0)(x)$ voor $f(x)=e^{\sin x}$ \\

$\left|\begin{array}{ll}
     & f(x)=e^{\sin x} \\
     \\
     & f'(x)=e^{\sin x}\cos x \\
     \\
     & f''(x)=e^{\sin x}\cos^2x-e^{\sin x}\sin x \\
\end{array}\right.$ \\

We gebruiken de bekende Taylorontwikkelingen rond $0$: \\

$ \sin x = x-\dfrac{x^3}{6}+\dfrac{x^5}{120} $ \\

$ e^u = 1+u+\dfrac{u^2}{2!}+\dfrac{u^3}{3!}+\dfrac{u^4}{4!}+\dfrac{u^5}{5!} $ \\

Met: \\

$ u = \sin x = x-\dfrac{x^3}{6}+\dfrac{x^5}{120} $ \\

Dus: \\

$ e^{\sin x}
= 1+\left(x-\dfrac{x^3}{6}+\dfrac{x^5}{120}\right)
+\dfrac{\left(x-\dfrac{x^3}{6}+\dfrac{x^5}{120}\right)^2}{2!} $ \\

$ \quad +\dfrac{\left(x-\dfrac{x^3}{6}+\dfrac{x^5}{120}\right)^3}{3!}
+\dfrac{\left(x-\dfrac{x^3}{6}+\dfrac{x^5}{120}\right)^4}{4!}
+\dfrac{\left(x-\dfrac{x^3}{6}+\dfrac{x^5}{120}\right)^5}{5!} $ \\

Tot en met graad $5$ geeft dit: \\

$ e^{\sin x}
= 1+x+\dfrac{x^2}{2}-\dfrac{x^4}{8}-\dfrac{x^5}{15} $ \\

Dus: \\

$ T_5(f,0)(x)=1+x+\dfrac{x^2}{2}-\dfrac{x^4}{8}-\dfrac{x^5}{15} $ \\
