Opgave: $\dint \dfrac{2x^2 - x + 1}{2x + 1} dx$\\

\begin{tabular}{lll|ll}
	$2x^2$  &	$-x$	&	$+1$	&	$2x$	&	$+1$	\\ \cline{4-5}
	$-2x^2$	&	$-x$	&			&	$x$		&	$-1$	\\ \cline{1-2}
			&	$-2x$	&	$+1$	&			&			\\
			&	$2x$	&	$+1$	&			&			\\ \cline{2-3}
			&			&	$2$		&			&			\\
\end{tabular}\\\\

$= \dint x - 1 + \dfrac{2}{2x + 1} dx = \dint x dx - \dint dx + 2\dint \dfrac{dx}{2x + 1}$\\

$\Rightarrow t = 2x + 1$, $t' = 2 \Rightarrow dx = \dfrac{dt}{2}$\\

$= \dfrac{x^2}{2} - x + \dint \dfrac{dt}{t} = \dfrac{x^2}{2} - x + \ln |t| + c$\\

$= \dfrac{x^2}{2} - x + \ln |2x + 1| + c$\\
