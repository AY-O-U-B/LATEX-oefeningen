$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{x^3}{\sqrt[3]{x^2 + 1}}dx$\\

$t=x^2+1 \Rightarrow dt=2x\,dx \Rightarrow x\,dx=\dfrac{1}{2}dt$\\

$x^2=t-1$\\

$\Rightarrow \displaystyle\int \dfrac{x^2 \cdot x}{\sqrt[3]{x^2+1}}dx=\displaystyle\int \dfrac{\left( t-1 \right)}{\sqrt[3]{t}} \cdot \dfrac{1}{2}dt$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int \dfrac{t-1}{\sqrt[3]{t}}dt$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int \left( t^{\tfrac{2}{3}}-t ^ {-\tfrac{1}{3}} \right)dt$\\

$=\dfrac{1}{2}\left( \dfrac{3}{5}t^{\tfrac{5}{3}}-\dfrac{3}{2}t ^{\tfrac{2}{3}} \right)+C$\\

$= \dfrac{3}{10}t^{\tfrac{5}{3}}-\dfrac{3}{4}t^{\tfrac{2}{3}}+C$\\

$= \dfrac{3}{10}\left( x^2+1 \right)^{\tfrac{5}{3}}-\dfrac{3}{4}\left( x^2+1 \right)^{\tfrac{2}{3}}+C$\\
