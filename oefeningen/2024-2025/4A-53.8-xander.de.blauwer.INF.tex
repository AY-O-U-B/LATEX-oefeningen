$ \displaystyle\int \sin^{-2}(x)\cos^{-4}(x)dx $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{\dfrac{\sin^2(x)}{\cos^2(x)} \cdot \cos^6(x)} = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{\tan^2(x)\cos^6(x)} $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\sec^6(x)}{\tan^2(x)}dx = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{(1+\tan^2(x))^2 \cdot \sec^2(x)}{\tan^2(x)}dx $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{(1 + 2\tan^2(x) + \tan^4(x)) \sec^2(x)} {\tan^2(x)}dx $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \left(\dfrac{1}{\tan^2(x)} + 2 + \tan^2(x)\right)\sec^2(x)dx $ \\

Stel $u = \tan(x) \Rightarrow du = \sec^2(x)dx $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int (u^{-2} + 2 + u^2)du $ \\
