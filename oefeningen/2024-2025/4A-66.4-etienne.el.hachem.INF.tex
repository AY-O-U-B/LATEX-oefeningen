$\displaystyle\int \sqrt{x^2 + 1}dx$ \\

$ \stackrel{\sqrt{x^2+1}= x+ t}= \displaystyle\int \left(-\dfrac{t^2-1}{2t}+t\right)\left(-\dfrac{t^2+1}{2t^2}\right)dt$ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \left(\dfrac{t^2+1}{2t}\right)\left(-\dfrac{t^2+1}{2t^2}\right)dt$ \\

$ = -\displaystyle\int \dfrac{(t^2+1)^2}{4t^3}dt$ \\

$ = -\dfrac{1}{4} \cdot \left( \displaystyle\int \dfrac{t^4+2t^2+1}{t^3} \right)dt$

$ = -\dfrac{1}{4} \cdot \left( \dfrac{t^2}{2}+2\cdot \ln |t| - \dfrac{1}{2t^2} \right) + c$ \\

$ = -\dfrac{(\sqrt{x^2+1}-x)^2}{8} - \dfrac{\ln |\sqrt{x^2+1}-x|}{2} + \dfrac{1}{8(\sqrt{x^2+1}-x)^2}+c$
