$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{5x^2+3x-2}{2x^3-3x^2+1}dx$\\

Vermits $2x^3-3x^2+1=(x-1)^2(2x+1)$:\\

$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{5x^2+3x-2}{(x-1)^2(2x+1)}dx$\\

$\dfrac{5x^2+3x-2}{2x^3-3x^2+1}=\dfrac{5x^2+3x-2}{(x-1)^2(2x+1)}=\dfrac{A}{(x-1)^2}+\dfrac{B}{(x-1)}+\dfrac{C}{2x+1}$\\

Voor $x = \dfrac{1}{2}$:\\

$C = \dfrac{5x^2+3x-2}{(x-1)^2} = -1$\\

Voor $x = 1$:\\

$A = \dfrac{5x^2+3x-2}{2x+1} = 2$\\

$\dfrac{5x^2+3x-2}{(x-1)^2(2x+1)}-\dfrac{2}{(x-1)^2}=\dfrac{5x^2+3x-2-2(2x+1)}{(x-1)^2(2x+1)} = \dfrac{5x^2-x-4}{(x-1)^2(2x+1)}$\\

$=\dfrac{(x-1)(5x+4)}{(x-1)^2(2x+1)}=\dfrac{(5x+4)}{(x-1)(2x+1)}$\\

Voor $x = 1$:\\

$B = \dfrac{5x+4}{2x+1} = 3$\\

Splitsing wordt dus:\\

$\dfrac{5x^2+3x-2}{2x^3-3x^2+1}=\dfrac{2}{(x-1)^2}+\dfrac{3}{(x-1)}-\dfrac{1}{2x+1}$\\

Bijgevolg is:\\

$I = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{2}{(x-1)^2}dx + \displaystyle\int \dfrac{3}{(x-1)}dx+ \displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{2x+1}dx$\\

$$t=x-1$$ $$dt=dx$$ $$u=2x+1$$ $$du=2dx$$\\

$= 2\displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{t^2}dt + 3\displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{t}dt - \dfrac{1}{2}\displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{u}dt$\\

$= -\dfrac{2}{x-1} + 3\ln|x-1| - \dfrac{1}{2}\ln|2x+1| + C$\\
