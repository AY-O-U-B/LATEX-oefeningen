$ \displaystyle\int x \sqrt{1 - 4x^2} dx $ \\

Stel $ t = 1 -4x^2 \Rightarrow dt = -8x dx $ \\

$ \Rightarrow x = \dfrac{\sqrt{t-1}}{4} \Rightarrow dx = \dfrac{dt}{-8x} $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\sqrt{\dfrac{t-1}{-4}} \sqrt{t}}{-8 \sqrt{\dfrac{t-1}{-4}}} dt $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\sqrt{t}}{-8} dt $ \\

$ = - \dfrac{1}{8} \displaystyle\int t^{\tfrac{1}{2}} dt $ \\

$ = - \dfrac{1}{8} \dfrac{t^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{\dfrac{3}{2}} + C $ \\

$ = - \dfrac{\sqrt{t^3}}{12} + C $ \\

$ = - \dfrac{\sqrt{(1 -4x^2)^3}}{12} + C $ \\
