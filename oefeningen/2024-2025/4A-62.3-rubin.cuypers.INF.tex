$I=\dint \dfrac{\sqrt{1-x^2}}{x} dx$\\

$\overset{x=\cos y }{=} \dint \dfrac{\sqrt{1-\cos^2y}}{\cos y} (-\sin y) dy$ \\

$=-\dint \dfrac{\sin^2 y}{\cos y} dy =-\dint \dfrac{1-\cos^2y}{\cos y}dy = -\dint \dfrac{1}{\cos y} dy+ \dint \cos y dy$\\

$=-\ln \left| \tan\left( \dfrac{y}{2} + \dfrac{\pi}{4} \right) \right|+ \sin y +C$\\

$=-\ln \left| \tan\left( \dfrac{y}{2} + \dfrac{\pi}{4} \right) \right| +\sqrt{1-\cos^2y} + C$\\

$\overset{y=\operatorname{Bgcos} x}{=} -\ln \left| \tan\left( \dfrac{\operatorname{Bgcos} x}{2} + \dfrac{\pi}{4} \right) \right| +\sqrt{1-x^2} + C$\\
