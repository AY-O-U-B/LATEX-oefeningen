$\displaystyle \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} \dfrac{dx}{1 + x^2}$ \\

$=\lim\limits_{a \rightarrow -\infty} \left( \displaystyle \int_a^0 \dfrac{dx}{1 + x^2}\right) + \lim\limits_{b \rightarrow +\infty} \left (\displaystyle \int_0^b \dfrac{dx}{1 + x^2} \right )$ \\

$=\lim\limits_{a \rightarrow -\infty} \left( \left [\operatorname{Bgtan} (x) \right ]_a^0 \right) + \lim\limits_{b \rightarrow +\infty} \left ( \left [ \operatorname{Bgtan} (x) \right]_0^b \right )$ \\

$=\lim\limits_{a \rightarrow -\infty} \left ( \bcancel{\operatorname{Bgtan} (0)} - \operatorname{Bgtan} (a) \right ) + \lim\limits_{b \rightarrow +\infty} \left (\operatorname{Bgtan} (b) - \bcancel{\operatorname{Bgtan} (0)} \right )$ \\ 

$= -\left ( \dfrac{-\pi}{2} \right ) + \dfrac{\pi}{2}$ \\

$= \pi$ \\
