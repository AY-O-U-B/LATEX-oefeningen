$I = \displaystyle\int \ln\left(\dfrac{x^2+1}{x^2-1}\right)dx$\\

Partiële integratie:\\

$\left\{ \begin{array}{l} 
    u = \ln\left(\dfrac{x^2+1}{x^2-1}\right)\\ 
    dv = dx 
\end{array} \right. 
\Rightarrow 
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} 
    du = \left(\dfrac{2x}{x^2+1}-\dfrac{2x}{x^2-1}\right)dx = \dfrac{-4x}{x^4-1}dx\\ 
    v = x
\end{array} \right.$\\

$ I = x\ln\left(\dfrac{x^2+1}{x^2-1}\right) - \displaystyle\int x \cdot \dfrac{-4x}{x^4-1}dx$\\

$ = x\ln\left(\dfrac{x^2+1}{x^2-1}\right)+ \displaystyle\int \dfrac{4x^2}{x^4-1}dx$\\

$ x^4-1 = (x-1)(x+1)(x^2+1)$\\

$ \dfrac{4x^2}{x^4-1}=\dfrac{A}{x-1}+\dfrac{B}{x+1}+\dfrac{Cx+D}{x^2+1}$\\

$ A = \left.\dfrac{4x^2}{(x+1)(x^2+1)}\right|_{x=1}= \dfrac{4}{2\cdot 2}=1$\\

$ B = \left.\dfrac{4x^2}{(x-1)(x^2+1)}\right|_{x=-1}= \dfrac{4}{-2\cdot 2}=-1$\\

$ Ci+D = \left.\dfrac{4x^2}{x^2-1}\right|_{x=i}= \dfrac{-4}{-2}=2$\\

$ \Rightarrow C=0,\quad D=2$\\

$ \dfrac{4x^2}{x^4-1}=\dfrac{1}{x-1}-\dfrac{1}{x+1}+\dfrac{2}{x^2+1}$\\

$ \displaystyle\int \dfrac{4x^2}{x^4-1}dx=\displaystyle\int\left(\dfrac{1}{x-1}-\dfrac{1}{x+1}+\dfrac{2}{x^2+1}\right)dx$\\

$ = \ln|x-1|-\ln|x+1|+2\operatorname{Bgtan}x + c$\\

$ = \ln\left|\dfrac{x-1}{x+1}\right|+2\operatorname{Bgtan}x+c$\\

Dus:\\

$ I =x\ln\left(\dfrac{x^2+1}{x^2-1}\right)+\ln\left|\dfrac{x-1}{x+1}\right|+2\operatorname{Bgtan}x+c$\\
