$\displaystyle\int \sqrt{2x}dx = \displaystyle\int (2x)^{\tfrac{1}{2}} dx$\\

$ = \displaystyle\int 2^{\tfrac{1}{2}} \cdot x^{\tfrac{1}{2}} dx$\\

$= 2^{\tfrac{1}{2}} \cdot  \displaystyle\int x^{\tfrac{1}{2}} dx$\\

$ = \sqrt{2} \displaystyle\int x^{\tfrac{1}{2}}dx$\\

$ = \sqrt{2} \cdot  \dfrac{x^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{\tfrac{3}{2}}$\\

$ = \dfrac{2\sqrt{2}\cdot \sqrt{x^3}}{3} = \dfrac{2x\sqrt{2x}}{3} +C$
