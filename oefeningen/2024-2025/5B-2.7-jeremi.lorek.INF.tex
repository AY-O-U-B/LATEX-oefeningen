$\displaystyle \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} \dfrac{x}{(x^2 + 4)^2} dx = \lim\limits_{a \to -\infty} \int_a^0 \dfrac{x}{(x^2 + 4)^2} dx + \lim\limits_{b \to +\infty} \int_0^b \dfrac{x}{(x^2 + 4)^2} dx$ \\

$\displaystyle \int \dfrac{x}{(x^2 + 4)^2} dx = \dfrac{1}{2} \int \dfrac{du}{u^2}$ \\

$u = x^2 + 4$ \\

$du = 2x dx$ \\

$\dfrac{1}{2} du = x dx$ \\

$\Rightarrow \displaystyle \int \dfrac{x}{(x^2 + 4)^2} dx = \dfrac{1}{2} \int \dfrac{du}{u^2} = \dfrac{-1}{2u} + C = \dfrac{-1}{2(x^2 + 4)} + C$ \\

$\displaystyle \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} \dfrac{x}{(x^2 + 4)^2} dx = \lim\limits_{a \to -\infty} \left[ \dfrac{-1}{2(x^2 + 4)} \right]_a^0 + \lim\limits_{b \to +\infty} \left[ \dfrac{-1}{2(x^2 + 4)} \right]_0^b$ \\

$= \lim\limits_{a \to -\infty} \left( \dfrac{-1}{8} + \dfrac{1}{2(a^2 + 4)} \right) + \lim\limits_{b \to +\infty} \left( \dfrac{-1}{2(b^2 + 4)} + \dfrac{1}{8} \right)$ \\

$= \dfrac{-1}{8} + 0 + 0 + \dfrac{1}{8} = 0$ \\
