\textbf{De cartesische coordinaten van} $\left(3, -\dfrac{5\pi}{6}\right)$; $(x, y) - ?$ \\\\

$\left \{\begin{array}{l} x = r\cos\theta \\ y = r\sin\theta \end{array}\right. \Rightarrow \left \{\begin{array}{l} x = 3\cos\left(-\dfrac{5\pi}{6}\right) \\ y = 3\sin\left(-\dfrac{5\pi}{6}\right) \end{array}\right.$ \\\\

$\Rightarrow \left(-\dfrac{5\pi}{6}\right)\left(\dfrac{180^\circ}{\pi}\right) = 150^\circ \Rightarrow 180^\circ - 150^\circ = 30^\circ \Rightarrow \left \{\begin{array}{l} \cos30^\circ = \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \\ \sin30^\circ = \dfrac{1}{2} \end{array}\right.$\\\\

$\Rightarrow x = 3\cos\left(-\dfrac{5\pi}{6}\right) = 3\left(-\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right) = -\dfrac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}$\\\\

$\Rightarrow y = 3\sin\left(-\dfrac{5\pi}{6}\right) = 3\left(-\dfrac{1}{2}\right) = -\dfrac{3}{2}$\\\\

\textit{\textbf{Resultaat:}} $\left(-\dfrac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}, -\dfrac{3}{2}\right)$
