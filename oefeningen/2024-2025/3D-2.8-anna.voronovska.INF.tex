$f(x) = \dfrac{x^2}{x-1}$\\

Domein: $\text{dom } f = \mathbb{R} \setminus \{1\}$\\

Afgeleide:\\

$ f'(x) = \dfrac{2x(x-1) - x^2 \cdot 1}{(x-1)^2} = \dfrac{2x^2 - 2x - x^2}{(x-1)^2} = \dfrac{x^2 - 2x}{(x-1)^2} = \dfrac{x(x-2)}{(x-1)^2} $\\

Nulpunten:\\

$x(x-2) = 0 \Leftrightarrow x = 0$ of $x = 2$\\
