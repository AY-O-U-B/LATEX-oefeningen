$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{2x^3 dx}{\sqrt{x^2 + 4}}$\\ 

$u = x^2 + 4$\\ 

$du = 2x dx$\\ 

$2x^3 dx=  x^2(2xdx)$\\

$x^2 = u - 4$\\ 

$\Rightarrow \displaystyle\int \dfrac{x^2(2xdx)}{\sqrt{x^2 + 4}}$\\ 

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{u - 4}{\sqrt{u}} du$\\ 

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{u}{\sqrt{u}} du - 4\displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{u}} du$\\ 

$= \displaystyle\int u^{1 - \tfrac{1}{2}} du - 4\displaystyle\int u^{-1/2} du$\\ 

$= \dfrac{2}{3}(x^2 + 4)^{\dfrac{3}{2}} - 8(x^2 + 4)^{\dfrac{1}{2}} + C$\\ 

$= \dfrac{2}{3}\sqrt{(x^2 + 4)^3} - 8\sqrt{x^2 + 4} + C$\\
