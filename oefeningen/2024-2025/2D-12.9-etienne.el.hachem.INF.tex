$\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow \infty} \left(\sqrt{2x^2+3} - x\right)$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow -\infty} \left(\sqrt{2x^2+3} - x\right) = +\infty - \left(-\infty\right) = +\infty$ \\

$\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow +\infty} \dfrac{\left(\sqrt{2x^2+3} - x\right) \left(\sqrt{2x^2+3} + x\right)}{\left(\sqrt{2x^2+3} + x\right)}$ \\
$ = \lim\limits_{x\rightarrow +\infty} \dfrac{2x^2 + 3 -x^2}{\left(\sqrt{2x^2+3} + x\right)}$ \\
$ = \lim\limits_{x\rightarrow +\infty} \dfrac{x^2 + 3}{\left(\sqrt{2x^2+3} + x\right)} = \lim\limits_{x\rightarrow +\infty} \dfrac{x^2}{(\sqrt{2} + 1)x} = +\infty$
