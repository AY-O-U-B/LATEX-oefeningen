$\displaystyle \int_0^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}} \sin 2x dx$ \\

$t = 2x \Rightarrow \dfrac{1}{2} dt = dx$ \\

$x = 0 \Rightarrow t = 0$ \\

$x = \dfrac{\pi}{2} \Rightarrow t = \pi$ \\

$\displaystyle \int_0^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}} \sin 2x dx = \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle \int_0^{\pi} \sin t dt$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \left[ -\cos t \right]_0^{\pi}$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \left( -\cos \pi + \cos 0 \right)$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \left( -(-1) + 1 \right)$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \cdot 2$ \\

$= 1$ \\

$\displaystyle \int_0^{\tfrac{\pi}{2}} \sin 2x dx = 1$ \\
