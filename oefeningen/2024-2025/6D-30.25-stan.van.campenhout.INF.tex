$ \displaystyle\sum\limits_{n = 2}^\infty n^2e^{-n^3} $\\

$ \displaystyle\int_0^{+\infty}n^2e^{-n^3}dn = -\dfrac{1}{3}\displaystyle\int_0^{+\infty}e^{-n^3}d\left(-n^3\right) $\\

$ = -\dfrac{1}{3}\left[e^{-n^3}\right]_0^{+\infty} = -\dfrac{1}{3}\left(\lim\limits_{a\rightarrow+\infty}\dfrac{1}{e^{a^3}} - \dfrac{1}{e^{0^3}}\right) $\\

$ = -\dfrac{1}{3}\left(0 - 1\right) = \dfrac{1}{3} \Rightarrow $ bestaat \\

$ \stackrel{\text{integraalcriterium}}{\Rightarrow} $ convergent \\
