$ I = \displaystyle\int_1^{+\infty} \dfrac{dx}{x\sqrt{x}}$\\
	
$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{x\sqrt{x}} = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{x^{\tfrac{3}{2}}} = \displaystyle\int x^{\tfrac{-3}{2}}dx = \dfrac{x^{\tfrac{-1}{2}}}{\dfrac{-1}{2}} +C = \dfrac{-2}{\sqrt{x}} + C$\\

	
$ I = \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow +\infty} \left[ \dfrac{-2}{\sqrt{x}} \right]_1^b = \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow +\infty} \left( \dfrac{-2}{\sqrt{b}} + 2\right) = 2 \Rightarrow$ Convergent\\
