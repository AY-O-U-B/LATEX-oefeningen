$ f(x) = x^2 $\\

$ a_n = \left< f | \cos(nx) \right>$\\

$= \dfrac{1}{\pi}\displaystyle\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}x^2\cos(nx)dx$\\

$= \dfrac{2}{\pi}\displaystyle\int_{0}^{\pi}x^2\cos(nx)dx$\\

$= \dfrac{2}{\pi}\left[ \dfrac{x^2\sin(nx)}{n} + \dfrac{2x\cos(nx)}{n^2} - \dfrac{2\sin(nx)}{n^3} \right]_0^\pi$\\

$= \dfrac{2}{\pi}\cdot\dfrac{2\pi(-1)^n}{n^2}$\\

$= \dfrac{4(-1)^n}{n^2} $\\

$ b_n = \left< f | \sin(nx) \right>
  = \dfrac{1}{\pi}\displaystyle\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}x^2\sin(nx)dx
  = 0 $\\

$ a_0 = \left< f | 1 \right>
  = \dfrac{1}{\pi}\displaystyle\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}x^2dx
  = \dfrac{2}{\pi}\displaystyle\int_{0}^{\pi}x^2dx
  = \dfrac{2}{\pi}\left[ \dfrac{x^3}{3} \right]_0^\pi
  = \dfrac{2\pi^2}{3} $\\

$ \Rightarrow \widehat{f}(x)
  = \dfrac{a_0}{2}
  + \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(a_n\cos(nx)+b_n\sin(nx)\right)
  = \dfrac{\pi^2}{3}
  + 4\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{(-1)^n}{n^2}\cos(nx) $\\
