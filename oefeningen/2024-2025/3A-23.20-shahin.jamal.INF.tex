$\left[\sec^3\left(\dfrac{1}{4}x\right)\right]'$\\

$= 3\sec^2\left(\dfrac{1}{4}x\right) \cdot \left(\sec\dfrac{1}{4}x\right)' = 3\sec^2\left(\dfrac{1}{4}x\right) \cdot \sin\left(\dfrac{1}{4}x\right) \cdot \sec^2\left(\dfrac{1}{4}x\right)$\\

$= \dfrac{3}{4}\sec^4\left(\dfrac{1}{4}x\right) \cdot \sin\left(\dfrac{1}{4}x\right)$\\
