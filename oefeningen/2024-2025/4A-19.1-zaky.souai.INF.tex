$I= \displaystyle \int e^x \sin (x) dx$\\

$\left\{\begin{array}{l}
u = \sin (x)\\
dv=e^x dx
\end{array}
\Rightarrow
\left\{\begin{array}{l}
du = \cos(x) dx\\
v=e^x
\end{array}\right.\right.$\\

$I= e^x\sin (x)- \displaystyle \int e^x\cos(x)dx$\\

$J= \displaystyle \int e^x\cos(x)dx$\\

$\left\{\begin{array}{l}
u = \cos (x)\\
dv=e^x dx
\end{array}
\Rightarrow
\left\{\begin{array}{l}
du = -\sin(x) dx\\
v=e^x
\end{array}\right.\right.$\\

$J= e^x\cos (x)- \displaystyle \int e^x(-\sin(x))dx$\\

$J= e^x\cos (x)+ \displaystyle \int e^x\sin(x)dx$\\

$J= e^x\cos (x)+I$\\

$I= e^x\sin (x)-J$\\

$I= e^x\sin (x)-\left(e^x\cos (x)+I\right)$\\

$I= e^x\sin (x)-e^x\cos (x)-I$\\

$2I= e^x\sin (x)-e^x\cos (x) + C$\\

$I= \dfrac{e^x}{2}\left(\sin (x)-\cos (x)\right)+C$
