$s_n = x_1\dfrac{1-q^n}{1-q}$\\

$\Leftrightarrow 31 = \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^{1-n}\cdot\dfrac{\left(1-\left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^n\right)}{\dfrac{1}{2}}$\\

$\Leftrightarrow 16 = \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^{1-n}$\\

$\Leftrightarrow 2^4 = 2^{n-1}$\\

$\Leftrightarrow n = 5$\\
