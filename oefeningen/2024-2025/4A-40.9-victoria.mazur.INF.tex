$ \displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{5-2x^2}} $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{(\sqrt{5})^2 - (\sqrt{2}x)^2}} $ \\

Stel $\sqrt{2}x = y \Rightarrow \sqrt{2}dx = dy \Leftrightarrow dx = \dfrac{dy}{\sqrt{2}}$ \\

$ I = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \displaystyle\int \dfrac{dy}{\sqrt{(\sqrt{5})^2 - y^2}} $ \\

$ = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \displaystyle\int \dfrac{dy}{\sqrt{5 - y^2}} $ \\

$ = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \cdot \dfrac{\operatorname{Bgsin}\left(\dfrac{y}{\sqrt{5}}\right)}{1} + C $ \\

$ = \dfrac{\operatorname{Bgsin}\left(\dfrac{y}{\sqrt{5}}\right)}{\sqrt{2}} + C $ \\

$ = \dfrac{\operatorname{Bgsin}\left(\dfrac{\sqrt{2}x}{\sqrt{5}}\right)}{\sqrt{2}} + C $ \\
