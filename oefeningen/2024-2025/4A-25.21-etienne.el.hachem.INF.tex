$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{x^2 + x + 1}{x - 1} dx$ \\

\begin{tabular}{lll|ll}
$x^2$ & $+x$ & $+1$ & $x$ & $-1$ \\ \cline{4-5}
$-x^2$ & $+x$ & & $x$ & $+2$ \\ \cline{1-2}
& $2x$ & $+1$ & & \\
& $-2x$ & $+2$ & & \\ \cline{2-3}
& & & $3$ & \\
\end{tabular} \\




$= \displaystyle\int (x+2)dx + \displaystyle\int \dfrac{3}{x-1} dx$ \\

Substitutie: $u = x - 1 \Rightarrow du = dx$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int (x + 2) dx + 3 \displaystyle\int \dfrac{du}{u}$ \\
