$ f(x) = \dfrac{2x^2-4x+1}{\sqrt{x}} $ \\

Toepassing quotient regel. \\

$ f'(x) = \dfrac{(4x-4) \cdot \sqrt{x} - (2x^2-4x+1) \cdot \dfrac{1}{2} x^{- \tfrac{1}{2}}}{x} $ \\

$ = \dfrac{4x^{\tfrac{3}{2}} - 4x^{\tfrac{1}{2}} - (x^{\tfrac{3}{2}} - 2x^{\tfrac{1}{2}} + \dfrac{1}{2} x^{-\tfrac{1}{2}})}{x} $ \\

$ = \dfrac{4x^{\tfrac{3}{2}} - 4x^{\tfrac{1}{2}} - x^{\tfrac{3}{2}} + 2x^{\tfrac{1}{2}} - \dfrac{1}{2} x^{-\tfrac{1}{2}}}{x} $ \\

$ = \dfrac{3x^{\tfrac{3}{2}} - 2x^{\tfrac{1}{2}} - \dfrac{1}{2} x^{-\tfrac{1}{2}} }{x} $ \\

$ = 3x^{\tfrac{1}{2}} - 2x^{-\tfrac{1}{2}} - \dfrac{1}{2} x^{-\tfrac{3}{2}} $ \\
