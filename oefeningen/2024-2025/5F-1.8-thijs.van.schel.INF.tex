$r = 2(1-\sin(\theta))$\\

$\dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int\limits_{\alpha}^{\beta} r^2d\theta$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int\limits_0^{2\pi} (2-\sin\theta)^2d\theta$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int\limits_0^{2\pi} 2^2 - 8\sin\theta + 4\sin^2\theta d\theta$\\

$ = 2\displaystyle\int\limits_0^{2\pi}d\theta - 4\displaystyle\int\limits_0^{2\pi}\sin\theta d\theta + 2\displaystyle\int\limits_0^{2\pi}\sin^2\theta d\theta$\\

$ = 2(2\pi) - 4(0) + 2\displaystyle\int\limits_0^{2\pi} \dfrac{1-\cos(2\theta)}{2}d\theta$\\

$ = 4\pi + 2\cdot\dfrac{1}{2}\displaystyle\int\limits_0^{2\pi}d\theta - 2\cdot\dfrac{1}{2}\displaystyle\int\limits_0^{2\pi} \cos(2\theta)d\theta$\\

$ = 4\pi + 2\left(\dfrac{1}{2}(2\pi-0)\right)$\\

$ = 4\pi + 2\pi = 6\pi$\\
