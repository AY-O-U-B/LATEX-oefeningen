$I = \displaystyle \int e^x \cos (x)dx$ \\

$\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     u = \cos (x) \\
     dv = e^xdx
\end{array}
\right .
\Rightarrow
\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     du = -\sin (x)dx \\
     v = e^x
\end{array}
\right .$ \\

$I = e^x \cos (x) + \displaystyle \int e^x \sin(x)dx$ \\

$\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     t = \sin (x) \\
     dw = e^xdx
\end{array}
\right .
\Rightarrow
\left \{
\begin{array}{l}
     dt = \cos (x)dx \\
     w = e^x
\end{array}
\right .$ \\

$I = e^x \cos (x) + e^x \sin (x) - \displaystyle \int e^x \cos(x)dx$ \\

$2I =  e^x \cos (x) + e^x \sin (x) + c$ \\

$I =  \dfrac{e^x \left( \cos (x) + \sin (x) \right)}{2} + c$ \\
