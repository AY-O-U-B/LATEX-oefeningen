$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{-x^2 + 4x - 3}}$ \\

$\sqrt{-x^2 + 4x - 3} = \sqrt{-(x - 2)^2 + 1}$ \\

Stel $u = x - 2$ \\

$\Rightarrow du = dx$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{-(x - 2)^2 + 1}}$ \\

$= \displaystyle\int \dfrac{du}{\sqrt{1 - u^2}}$ \\

$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{du}{\sqrt{1 - u^2}} = \Bgsin(u) + C$ \\

$\Bgsin(u) = \Bgsin(x - 2)$ \\

$\Rightarrow \displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{-x^2 + 4x - 3}} = \Bgsin(x - 2) + C$ \\
