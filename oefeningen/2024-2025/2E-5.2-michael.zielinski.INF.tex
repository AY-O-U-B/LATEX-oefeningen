$
  \lim\limits_{x \to 0}
  \left(1 - x\right)^{\tfrac{a}{x}}
$\\

Substitutie $u = -x$\\

$u \to 0$ als $x \to 0$\\

$\left(1 - x\right)^{\tfrac{a}{x}}
      =\left(1 + u\right)^{-\tfrac{a}{u}}
      =\left(\left(1 + u\right)^{\tfrac{1}{u}}\right)^{-a}$\\

Bekende limiet:\\

$
  \lim\limits_{u \to 0}
  \left(1 + u\right)^{\tfrac{1}{u}}
  = e$\\

Dus\\

$
  \lim\limits_{x \to 0}
  \left(1 - x\right)^{\tfrac{a}{x}}
  = e^{-a}$\\
