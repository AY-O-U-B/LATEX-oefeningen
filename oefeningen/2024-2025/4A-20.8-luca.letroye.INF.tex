$\displaystyle\int \cosh^2x dx$\\

$ = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\cosh(2x)+1}{2} dx$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int \cosh(2x)+1 dx$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2}\left(\displaystyle\int\cosh(2x)dx + \displaystyle\int1dx\right)$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{2}\left(\dfrac{\sinh(2x)}{2}+x\right)$\\

$ = \dfrac{\sinh(2x)}{4} +\dfrac{1}{2}x +C$
