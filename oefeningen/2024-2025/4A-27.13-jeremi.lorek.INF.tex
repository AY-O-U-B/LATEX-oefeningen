$\displaystyle \int \dfrac{x + 2}{x^2 + 4x + 7} dx$ \\

$= \displaystyle \int \dfrac{x + 2}{(x + 2)^2 + 3} dx$ \\

Stel $u = x + 2 \Rightarrow du = dx$\\

$= \displaystyle \int \dfrac{u}{u^2 + 3} du$ \\

Stel $t = u^2 + 3 \Rightarrow dt = 2u du \Rightarrow \dfrac{1}{2} dt = u du $\\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle \int \dfrac{dt}{t}$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \ln |t| + C = \dfrac{1}{2} \ln |u^2 + 3| + C$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \ln \left| (x + 2)^2 + 3 \right| + C$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \ln \left| x^2 + 4x + 7 \right| + C$ \\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \ln (x^2 + 4x + 7) + C$ \\
