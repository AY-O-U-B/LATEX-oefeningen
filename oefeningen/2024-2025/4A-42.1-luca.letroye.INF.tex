$\displaystyle\int \dfrac{dx}{x(x-2)} = \dfrac{A}{x} + \dfrac{B}{(x-2)}$\\

$\left. \dfrac{1}{x}\right|_{x=2}=\dfrac{1}{2} = B$\\

$\left. \dfrac{1}{(x-2)}\right|_{x=0}=\dfrac{-1}{2} = A$\\

$\Rightarrow \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\dfrac{-1}{2}dx}{x}+ \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\dfrac{1}{2}dx}{x}$\\

$= -\dfrac{1}{2}\ln|x| + \dfrac{1}{2}\ln|x-2| + C$\\
