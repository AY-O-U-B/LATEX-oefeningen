$r = \sin^3 \left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right)$ in het interval $[0, 3\pi]$\\

$r^2 = \sin^6 \left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right)$\\

$(r')^2 = \sin^4 \left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right)\cdot \cos^2 \left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right)$\\

$\Rightarrow \dint_\alpha^\beta \sqrt{r^2 + (r')^2} d\theta = \dint_0^{3\pi} \sqrt{\sin^6 \left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right) + \sin^4 \left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right) \cdot \cos^2 \left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right)} d\theta$\\

$= \dint_0^{3\pi} \sqrt{\sin^4 \left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right)\left(\sin^2 \left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right) + \cos^2 \left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right)\right)} d\theta$\\

$= \dint_0^{3\pi} \sqrt{\sin^4\left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right)} d\theta$\\

$= \dint_0^{3\pi}\sin^2 \left(\dfrac{\theta}{3}\right) d\theta $\\

$= \dfrac{1}{2} \dint_0^{3\pi} \left(1 + \cos \left(\dfrac{2\theta}{3}\right)\right) d\theta$\\

$= \left[\dfrac{\theta}{2}\right]_0^{3\pi} + \dfrac{3}{4}\left[\sin\left(\dfrac{2\theta}{3}\right)\right]_0^{3\pi}$\\

$= \dfrac{3\pi}{2}$\\
