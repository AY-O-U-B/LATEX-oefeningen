$ \displaystyle\int\limits_{-1}^1\dfrac{1}{x^2}dx = \displaystyle\int\limits_{-1}^0\dfrac{1}{x^2}dx + \displaystyle\int\limits_0^1\dfrac{1}{x^2}dx
= \lim\limits_{a\rightarrow0^-}\displaystyle\int\limits_{-1}^a\dfrac{1}{x^2}dx + \lim\limits_{b\rightarrow0^+} \displaystyle\int\limits_b^1\dfrac{1}{x^2}dx $\\

$ = -\lim\limits_{a\rightarrow0^-}\left[\dfrac{1}{x}\right]_{-1}^a-\lim\limits_{b\rightarrow0^+}\left[\dfrac{1}{x}\right]_b^1 
= -\left(-\infty+1\right)-\left(-\infty+1\right) = +\infty $\\
