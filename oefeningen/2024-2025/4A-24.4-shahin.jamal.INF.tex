$\displaystyle\int\cos(\ln x) dx$\\

$\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       u = \cos(\ln x)\\
       dv = dx
   \end{array}
\right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       du = \dfrac{-\sin(\ln x)}{x}dx \\
       v = x
   \end{array}
\right.$\\

$\Rightarrow x\cos(\ln x) + \displaystyle\int x \cdot \dfrac{\sin(\ln x)}{x} dx$\\

$\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       u = \sin(\ln x)\\
       dv = dx
   \end{array}
\right.
\Rightarrow
\left\{
   \begin{array}{l}
       du = \dfrac{\cos(\ln x)}{x} \\
       v = x
   \end{array}
\right.$\\

$\Rightarrow x \cos(\ln x) + x\sin(\ln x) - \displaystyle\int x\cdot \dfrac{\cos(\ln x)}{x} dx$\\

Breng $\displaystyle\int \cos(\ln x) dx$ naar het linkerlid\\

$2\displaystyle\int \cos(\ln x)dx = x\cos(\ln x) + x\sin(\ln x)+c$\\

$\Rightarrow \displaystyle\int \cos(\ln x)dx = \dfrac{x}{2}\cos(\ln x) + \dfrac{x}{2}\sin(\ln x) + c$\\
