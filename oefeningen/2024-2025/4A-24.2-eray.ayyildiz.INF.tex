$I = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\ln^2 x}{x} dx $\\

$\left\{
    \begin{array}{l}
        u = \ln^2 x \\
        dv = \dfrac{1}{x}  dx
    \end{array}
\right .
\Rightarrow
\left\{
    \begin{array}{l}
        du = 2 \dfrac{\ln x}{x} \\
        v = \ln x
      
    \end{array}
\right .$\\

$I = \ln x \cdot \ln^2 x - 2 \displaystyle\int \dfrac{\ln^2 x}{x} dx $\\

$I = \ln x \cdot \ln^2 x - 2 I + c$\\

$3I = \ln x \cdot \ln^2 x + c $\\

$I = \dfrac{\ln x \cdot \ln^2 x}{3} + c $\\
