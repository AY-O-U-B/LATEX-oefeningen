$s_n = 1 S_2(n) + 2 S_1(n)$\\

$ = \dfrac{1}{3}n^3 + \dfrac{1}{2}n^2 + \dfrac{1}{6}n + 2 \left( \dfrac{1}{2}n^2 + \dfrac{1}{2}n \right)$\\

$ = \dfrac{2x^3 + 9n^2 + 7n}{6}$\\

$s_n \in \mathbb{N} \Leftrightarrow 2x^3 + 9n^2 + 7n = h \equiv 0 (\operatorname{mod} 6) $\\

$n \equiv 0 (\operatorname{mod} 6) \Rightarrow h \equiv 0 (\operatorname{mod} 6)$\\

$n \equiv 1 (\operatorname{mod} 6) \Rightarrow h \equiv 2+9+7 \equiv 18 \equiv 0 (\operatorname{mod} 6)$\\

$n \equiv 2 (\operatorname{mod} 6) \Rightarrow h \equiv 1+36+14 \equiv 66 \equiv 0 (\operatorname{mod} 6)$\\

$n \equiv 3 (\operatorname{mod} 6) \Rightarrow h \equiv 54+81+21 \equiv 156 \equiv 0 (\operatorname{mod} 6)$\\

$n \equiv 4 (\operatorname{mod} 6) \Rightarrow h \equiv 128+144+28 \equiv 300 \equiv 0 (\operatorname{mod} 6)$\\

$n \equiv 5 (\operatorname{mod} 6) \Rightarrow h \equiv 250 + 225 + 35 \equiv 510 \equiv 0 (\operatorname{mod} 6)$\\

dus $2x^3 + 9n^2 + 7n \equiv 0 (\operatorname{mod} 6) $\\

dus $s_n \in \mathbb{N}$\\

QED\\
