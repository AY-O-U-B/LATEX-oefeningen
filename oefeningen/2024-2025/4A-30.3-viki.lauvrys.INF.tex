$ \displaystyle\int x^3 \sqrt{1 - x^2} dx $ \\

Stel $ t = 1 -x^2 \Rightarrow dt = -2x dx $ \\

$ \Rightarrow x = \sqrt{1 -t} \Rightarrow dx = \dfrac{dt}{-2x} $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \left( \sqrt{1 -t} \right)^3 \sqrt{t} \dfrac{dt}{-2(\sqrt{1 -t})} $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{(1 -t) \sqrt{1-t} \sqrt{t}}{-2 \left( \sqrt{1-t} \right)} dt $ \\

$ = \displaystyle\int \dfrac{(1 -t) \sqrt{t}}{-2} dt $ \\

$ = - \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int \left( \sqrt{t} - \sqrt{t^3} \right) dt $ \\

$ = - \dfrac{1}{2} \displaystyle\int \left( t^{\tfrac{1}{2}} - t^{\tfrac{3}{2}} \right) dt $ \\

$ = \dfrac{-t^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{3} + \dfrac{t^{\tfrac{5}{2}}}{5} + C $ \\

$ = \dfrac{-(1 - x^2)^{\tfrac{3}{2}}}{3} + \dfrac{(1 - x^2)^{\tfrac{5}{2}}}{5} +  C$ \\
