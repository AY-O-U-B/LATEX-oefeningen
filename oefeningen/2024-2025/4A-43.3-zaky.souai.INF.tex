$\displaystyle I=\int \dfrac{4x^2-6x+1}{2x^3-x^2}\,dx$\\

$\displaystyle \dfrac{4x^2-6x+1}{x^2(2x-1)}=\dfrac{A}{x^2}+\dfrac{B}{x}+\dfrac{C}{2x-1}$\\

$\displaystyle A=\dfrac{4x^2-6x+1}{2x-1}|_{x=0}=\dfrac{1}{-1}=-1$\\

$\displaystyle \dfrac{4(x-1)}{x(2x-1)}=\dfrac{B}{x}+\dfrac{C}{2x-1}$\\

$\displaystyle B=\dfrac{4(x-1)}{2x-1}|_{x=0} = 4$\\

$\displaystyle C=\dfrac{4(x-1)}{x}|_{x=\dfrac{1}{2}}=-4$\\

$\displaystyle I=\int\left(-\dfrac{1}{x^2}+\dfrac{4}{x}-\dfrac{4}{2x-1}\right)dx$\\

$\displaystyle I=\dfrac{1}{x}+4\ln|x|-2\ln|2x-1|+C$
